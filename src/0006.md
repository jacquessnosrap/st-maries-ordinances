# Ordinance 6

An Ordinance prohibiting, disorderly conduct, defining what shall be considered such, and providing for penalties for violation thereof.

Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Villages of St Maries

## Section 1
That any person or persons, who shall be guilty of any violent riotous or disorder by conduct, or who shall use any profane, abusive or obsene language, in any street, house or place, whereby the peace or quiet of the village is or may be disturbed, or who shall in public, be guilty of any indecent or immoral act or practice in said Village or shall be found in a helpless condition upon any street or public place, in this Village through intoxication, shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and shall upon conviction before any competent court be fined not less than FIVE nor more than TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS, or be imprisoned in the Village jail not less than five nor more than twenty five days, or both fine and imprisonment, at the discretion of the court. 

## Section 2
That any person or persons who, shall fire off or discharge any ordinance, gun, pistol, rifle, or other 
firearm within the corporate limits of the Village shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof before any justice of the peace, having jurisdiction shall be fined, not to exceed FIFTY DOLLARS provided, that upon national holidays and other days of public celebration the Board of Trustees may permit under such regulations as they deem bestaria , the firing of such ordinance, and other firearms. Provided further that the Board of Trustees may issue permits for the running of any shooting gallery, if they deem best for public interests.

## Section 3
That any person or persons who shall be guilty of any assault, or of any battery, within the limits of the Village shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof, shall be fined not less than ONE nor more than FIFTY DOLLARS, or by imprisonment not less than one nor more than fifty days, or by both fine and imprisonment, at the discretion of the court.

## Section 4
Any person or persons who shall draw any species of a firearm, or any dirk, dagger, or knife, or other deadly or dangerous weapon in threatening, menacing or insolent manner, with the intent, of using the same upon the person of another, or threatening to so use said weapon, or in a manner which shall cause any person to fear that it may be used upon his person, such person shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and upon conviction there of shall be fined not less than TWENTY FIVE and not to exceed ONE HUNDRED DOLLARS.

## Section 5
Any person or persons who shall resist any peace officer, or shall refuse to assist him in the discharge of his duties, or who shall by any means aid or assist any person in custody upon a charge of violating any of the Ordinances of the Village in his endeavors to escape from such custody, whether such escape be effected or not, shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof to shall be fined not less than ONE and not more than FIFTY DOLLARS, or by imprisonment in the Village jail not lass than one day, nor more than fifty days or by both fine and imprisonment, at the, discretion of the court. 

## Section 6
Any person who shall throw or deposit in any street, sidewalk or footpath of this Village any broken glass bottles, crockery, nails or other substance whatever whereby the feet of horses, pedestrians or any beast of burden, may be injured or shall throw, deposit of sweep into or upon any street sidewalk or footpath of the Village any paper or other substance whatever except snow or dirt resulting from travel shall be deemed guilt of a misdemenor and upon conviction thereof shall be fined not less than ONE and not more than TEN DOLLARS.

## Section 7
Any person who shall cruelly beat, torture, misuse, deprive of food and water or otherwise treat any animal with cruelty within this corporation limit of the Villages shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof shall be fined not less than five nor more than twenty five dollars. 

## Section 8
If any person or persons shall introduce or take into, the Village jail or deliver to any prisoner confined therein, any wine spiritous or malt liquors, except by permission of the marshal or upon the prescription of a practicing physician he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof shall be fined not less than FIVE nor more than TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS or by imprisonment, or by both fine and imprisonment at the discretion of the court.

## Section 9 
Any person under the age of sixteen who goes into or frequents at any time any saloon or place where liquor is sold, or into any gambling house except by the permission of the parent or guardian of such child, or for any person to entice or persuade any such child to enter or frequent auch place or places, any person found violating the provisions of the section shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and on conviction thereof it be punished by fine in any sum not exceeding fifty dollars or by imprisonment in the city jail not exceeding ten days, or by both such fine and imprisonment at the discretion of the court.

## Section 10
No bawdy house, house of ill-fame or disorderly house shall be kept or maintained within this city; and every person who shall set up, open or keep such house, or shall be an inmate of or in any way connected with such house, and every person who shall permit any tenement, building or premises in his or her possession, or under his or her control to be used as any such house shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and upon conviction thereof, shall be punished by a fine not less than five dollars or more than fifty dollars, or by imprisonment not less than five days nor more than twenty days, or by both such fine and imprisonment for each offense and it shall be the duty of the Village Marshal and his deputies, and all police officers and night watchmen of the Village, either upon their knowledge, information or belief, or upon the complaint of any citizen, to arrest and take before a Magistrate for trial any person who shall violate any of the provisions of this section, and any such officers who having knowledge of the violation of any of the provisions of this section, shall neglect or fail so to do, shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor in office, and upon conviction of such neglect or failure, shall be puhished by a fined of not less than five nor more than fifty dollars. 
In all cases of prosecutions under Section 10 of this ordinance, common fame shall be competent evidence in supporting of the complaint, and every house or place used or occupied for purposes of prostitution, fornication, assignation or lewdness, shall be taken and deemed to be a bawdy house within the meaning of this ordinance, in any prosecution against any person or persons for violating any of the provisions of this ordinance. 

## Section 11
In all cases of conviction for any of the offenses mentioned in this ordinance the court shall adjudge the offender to pay the costs of the prosecution and shall in default of the payment of both fine and costs, commit the offender to the Village jail for a term of imprisonment not exceeding ninety days, and in all cases when the term of imprisonment in the Village jail in lieu of fine, the offender shall be imprisoned in default of payment of fine, one day for every two dollars of such fine. 

Approved November 6, 1902.
