AN ORDINANCE CREATING AND ESTABLISHING "LOCAL IMPROVEMENT DISTRICT NO. 3". CITY OF ST. MARIES, BENEWAH COUNTY, IDAHO, DESIGNATING THE PROPERTY INCLUDED THEREIN, NAMING THE STREETS AND AVENUES THEREIN AND DESIGNATING THE POINTS BETWEEN WHICH SAID IMPROVEMENTS ARE TO BE MADE: 

PROVIDING FOR THE IMPROVEMENT OF SAID PORTIONS OF SAID STREETS AND AVENUES BY GRADING THEM AND CURBING THEM WITH CONCRETE CURBING AND PUTTING IN THE NECESSARY CROSS - WALKS, LOWERING THE WATER PIPES WHERE NECESSARY TO PERMIT OF SAID GRADING AND RAISING OR LOWERING THE SEWER MANHOLES WHERE NECESSARY TO PERIT OF SAID GRADING, MOVING SUCH TELEPHONE OR ELECTRIC LIGHT POLES WHERE IT ILAY BE NECESSARY TO MOVE TO PERMIT OF SAID GRADING AND MAKING SUCH OTHER INCIDENTAL IMPROVEMENTS AS MAY BE NECESSARY TO PERMIT OF SAID GRADING;

PROVIDING HOW AND THE MANNER IN WHICH SAID IMPROVEMENTS SHALL BE MADE, ORDERING THE MAKING OF SAID IM- PROVEMENTS AND PROVIDING THAT THE COST AND EXPENSE THEREOF, EXCLUD- ING THAT PORTION TO BE PAID BY THE CITY AT LARGE, BUT INCLUDING THE EXPENSE OF THE IMPROVEMENTS IN THE SPACE FORMED BY THE JUNCTION OF TWO OR MORE STREETS AND THE EXPENSE OF THE IMPROVEMENTS IN THE SPACE FORMED BY THE INTERSECTION OF THE STREETS AND THE EXPENSE OF THE IMPROVEMENTS IN THE SPACE WHEREIN ONE MAIN STREET TERMINATES IN OR CROSSES ANOTHER MAIN STREET AND THE EXPENSE OF THE IMPROVEMENTS IN THE SPACE OPPOSITE ALLEYS AND THE EXPENSE OF ALL THE STREET CROSSINGS OR CROSS-WALKS, SHALL BE TAXED AND ASSESSED UPON ALL THE PROPERTY IN SAID IMPROVEMENT DISTRICT;

PROVIDING THE MANNER OF THE ASSESSMENT OF SUCH COST AND EXPENSE UPON THE PROPERTY MADE LIABLE THEREFOR;

PROVIDING WHAT PORTION OF THE COST OF SAID IM- PROVEONTS SHALL BE PAID BY THE CITY OF ST. MARIES AND PROVIDING WHAT PORTION OF THE COST OF SAID IMPROVELENTS SHALL BE PAID BY THE PROPERTY WITHIN SAID DISTRICT;

PROVIDING THAT THE COST AND EXPENSE OF SAID WORK SO ASSESSED ON THE LOTS AND LANDS IN SAID LOCAL IMPROVEMENT DISTRICT SHALL BECOME A LIEN UPON THE SAME AND PROVIDING FOR THE MANNER OF THE FORECLOSURE OF SUCH LIENS;

PROVIDING FOR THE PREPARING OF AN ASSESSMENT ROLL AND DEFINING THE DUTIES AND POWERS OF THE STREET COMMITTEE AND CITY ENGINEER IN CONNECTION THEREWITH;

PROVIDING FOR THE MANNER IN WHICH SAID ASSESSMENT ROLL SHALL BE MADE, ITS CORRECTION, REVISION, CONFIRM- ATION, AND FOR THE MAKING OF A NEW ASSESSMENT ROLL IN CASE THE SAKE BECOMES NECESSARY;

PROVIDING FOR THE PAYMENT OF THE COST AND EXPENSE OF SAID IMPROVEMENTS WHICH ARE CHARGEABLE TO THE ABUTTING, CONTIGUOUS, ADJOINING, OR APPROXIMATE PROPERTY OF TEN EQUAL ANNUAL INSTALLMENTS AND AUTHORIZING AND PROVIDING FOR THE ISSUANCE IN THE NAME OF THE CITY OF ST. MARIES OF SPECIAL ASSESSMENT IMPROVEMENT BONDS OF SAID LOCAL IMPROVEMENT DISTRICT AND PROVIDING FOR THE COLLECTION OF SAID SPECIAL TAX ASSESSMENT;

AND PROVIDING FOR THE REDEMPTION OF SAID BONDS;

AND PROVIDING FOR THE REPEAL OF ALL ORDINANCES AND PARTS OF ORDINANCES IN COMFLICT WITH THES ORDINANCE.

WHEREAS, there has been presented to the City Council of the City of St. Maries, Idaho, a petition praying for the formation and establishment of a local improvement district to include the streets and avenues horeinafter described and the property contiguous, abutting and tributary thereto, and praying that in addition to the other costs and expenses of the work done in such improvement district the cost and expense of all improvements in the spaces formed by the junction of two or more streets, or wherein one main street terminates in or crosses another main street, and also the cost and expense of all street crossings or cross-walks be assessed against all the property in the improvement district in proportion to the benefits derived,

And Whereas said petition was and is signed by seventy (70) per cent of the resident owners of property within such improvement district subject to assessment for the work in such district, NOW THEREFORE,

BE IT ORDAINED by the Mayor and City Council of the City of St. Maries, Benewah County, Idaho:-

## Section 1

That there is hereby established, "Local Improve- ment District No. 3" of the City of St. Maries, Benewah County, Idaho, which district includes all the property fronting or abutt- ing on, contiguous or tributary to the streets to be improved between the Ponis named and designated in Ordinance 170, which Ordinance No. 170 was duly passed by the City Council of the City of St. Maries on the 9th day of July, A. D., 1915, and approved by the Mayor of said City on said date and thereafter published in the official newspaper of said City in accordance with the laws of the State of Idaho; said lands affected and included therein to extend from the said designated portions of said streets and avenues back from such streets and avenues, if platted in blocks, to the center of the blocks, if platted in lots only to the center of the lots; and if not platted, to a distance of 125 feet from the street or avenue upon which said lots or lands face or abutt.

That the streets and avenues of said City and the portions thereof to be improved as named and designated in said Ordinance No. 170, and to which the property included in said Improvement District is abutting, contiguous or fronts on, are as follows, to-wit:-

Fourteenth Street from the South Boundary Line of the City of St. Maries to the South Line of College Avenue; Thirteenth Street from the South Boundary Line of the City of St. Maries Twelfth Street from the to the South line of College Avenue; South Boundary Line of the City of St. Maries to the South line of College Avenue; Eleventh Street from the South Boundary Line of the City of St. Maries to the Couth Line of College Avenue; Tenth Street from the South Boundary Line of the City of St. Maries to the South Line of College Avenue; Ninth Street from the South Boundary Line of the City of St. Maries to the South Line of College Avenue; College Avenue from the West line of Tenth Street to the East Line of Fourteenth Street; Jefferson Avenue from the West Line of Winth Street to the East Line of Fourteenth Street; Washington Avenue from the West Line of Ninth Street to the East Line of Fourteenth Street.

Section 2. That the general character of the improvements to be med upon the aforesaid portions of said streets and avenues in said "Local Improvement District No. 3" shall consist of grading them, curbing them with concrete curbing and putting in the necessary cross-walks, lowering the water pipes where necessary to permit of said grading, and raising or lowering the Bewer manholes where necessary to rermit of said grading; putting in retaining walls where necessary to permit of said grading; em by moving such telephone and electric light poles as it may be necessary to move to permit of said grading, and by making nhatever other incidental improvements that may be necessary to permit of said grading.

All of said improvements to be under the supervision of the accordance with Ordinance No. 170 of the City of St. Maries; City Engineer and City Council of the City of St. Maries and in said grading on said streets and avenues to conform to the grades

ordinance of said heretoforofiles thereof now on file in the office of the City Engineer of said City, and all of said improvements shall be made to conform to the plans and specifications of said improvements the office of the Engineer of said City and

adopted and approved by the City Council thereof.

Section 3. That said improvements shall be and hereby are ordered made and that the cost and expense thereof, including the cost and expense of the improvements in the space formed by the junction of two or more streets and the expense of the im- provements in the space formed by the intersection of streets and the expense of the improvements in the space wherein one main street terminates in or crosses another main street and the expense of the improvements in the space opposite alleys and the expense of all the street crossings or cross-walks shall be taxed and assessed upon all the property in said "Local Improvement Dis- trict No. 3", except the cost and expense of lowering the water mains and pipes where necessary to permit of said grading and the cost and expense required to raise or lower sewer manholes where necessary to permit of said grading, which last named cost shall be paid by the City of St. St. Maries.

Where any work for the improvement of any street or avenue mentioned in this ordinance is done or made on one side of the center line of said portion of said streets or avenues, the lots or portions of lots or parcels of land fronting on that side only shall be assessed to cover the expense of said improvements.

Section 4. The cost and expense of said improvements, except the portion thereof to be paid by the city, shall be assessed upon all the property in said "Local Improvement District No. 3" in proportion to the number of feet of such lands or lots fronting on said portions of said streetsor abutting, contiguous or tributary thereto, and in proportion to the benefits derived from said improvement, and the expense and cost of said work so assessed on said lands shall become a lien upon upon said lands, which shall take precedence of all other liens and which may be foreclosed in accordance with the provisions of the code of Civil Procedure of the State of Idaho.

Such suit shall be in the name of the City of St. Maries, as plaintiff and in such proceedings where the court trying the same shall be satisfied that the work has been done or material furnished, which, according to the true intent of this Section would be properly chargeable on such lot or lands through or by which the street or avenue improved may pass, a recovery shall be permitted, or a charge enforced to the extent of the proper proportion of the value of the work or material which would be chargeable on such lot or lands notwithstanding any informatities, irregularities or defects in any of the proceedings of this city or through any of its officers.

Section 5. That upon the passage of this ordinance the street and alley committee, together with the City Engineer shall make out an assessment roli according to the provisions of this ordinance and of Chapter 5 of the Political Code of the State of Idaho as amended by Chapter 81 of the Session Laws of 1911, rela- ting to the "powers of cities and villages" approved on March 13, 1911, and as further amended by Chapter 97 of the Session Laws of 1915, relating to "powers of cities and villages", approved March 15, 1915, and shall certify the same to the Council of said City of St. Maries. Said assessment roll shall, among other things, contain a list of all property fronting on or abutting, contiguous or tributary to the said portions of the said streets and avenues to be improved and included in said improvement dis- trict, together with the name of the owner or owners of such lots, pieces or parcels of land which are so assessed, if known, the description of said lots, pieces or parcels of land and the amount of assessment levied upon each of such lots, pieces or parcels of land, which assessment shall be made to cover the entire cost and expense of said improvements of said district, except the portion thereof to be paid by the City as herein provided, in proportion to the number of feet of such lands and lots fronting on or abutting, contiguous or tributary to the por-

tions of said streets or avenues to be improved, and in proportion to the benefits derived by said improvements.

Section 6. Upon receiving the said assessment roll the said City Clerk of said City of St. Maries shall give notice, by three successive publications in the official newspaper of said city, that said assessment roll is on file in his office, stating the date of filing same, and said notice shall state a time at which the City Council will hear and consider objections to said assess- ment roll by parties aggreived by such assessment.

The owner or owners of any property which is assessed in such assessment roll, whether named or not in such roll, may, within ten days from the last publication provided herein, file with the Clerk his or their objections, in writing, to said assessment or any por- tion thereof, which objection must be definite and specific as to the property or piece of property being included in said assess- ment roll to which said objection is made, as well as to the speci- fic grounds for such objection.

Section 7. At the time appointed for hearing objections to such assessment the City Council shall determine all ob- jections which shall have been filed by any party interested, to the regularity of the proceedings in making such assessment, am to the correctness of such assessment, or of the amount levied on any particular lot or parcel of land, and the said City Council shall have the power to adjourn such hearing from time to time and shall have the power, in their discretion to revise, correct, con- firm or set aside such assessment and to order that such assess- ment be made denova, and said City Council shall pass an order approving and confirming said assessment as corrected by them, and their decision and order shall be a final determination of the rogularity, validity and correctness of said assessment to the amount thereof levied on each lot and parcel of land.

Section 8. Any person who shall file objections to such as hereinbefore provided, shall have assessment or re-assessment the right to appeal to the District Court of the Eighth Judicial District of the State of Idaho, in and for the County of Benewah, Such appeal shall be taken as is provided by Section 9 of Section 2238F of the Revised Codes of the State of Idaho as contained in Chapter 97 of the Session Laws of 1915.
