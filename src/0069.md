An ordinance providing that all able bodied male residents of the Village of St. Maries, between the ages of 21 and Fifty years shall perform two days labor upon the Streets, alleys and public highways of said Village: providing for a commutation fee in lieu of said labor, and providing a penalty for the violation of this ordinance. 

Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries:

## Section 1

That all the able bodied male residents of the Village of St. Maries, Idaho, between the ages of Twenty one and Fifty Years, shall between the first day of April and the First day of September of each year either by themselves or a satisfactory substitute, perform Two days labor upon the Streets, alleys or highways of said Village within the corporate limits of said Village at such time as the proper officer may direct and upon three days notice given in writing by such officer to appear at a time and place specified in said notice to perform said labor.

## Section 2

Any person so desiring may pay to the proper officer the sum of four dollars as a commutation fee in lieu of said labor if he so desires upon receipt of notice to perform said labor, which sum is hereby fixed as the commutation fee to be paid by all persons subject to perform said labor in lieu thereof in case they desire to do so.

## Section 3

That all persons who fail to appear and perform the labor aforesaid after being notified so to do, or to pay the commutation fee in lieu thereof shall forfeit the sum of four dollars per day for each days delinquency as provided by law which sum if not paid to the said Village may be certified to the tax collector and collected as other taxes.

## Section 3

The Village clerk shall when so directed by the Board of Trustees certify to the tax collector a list of the persons liable for said tax and penalty with the amount due from each person as provided by law for collection.

Passed and approved this 24 day of June A.D. 1909.
Perley Martin Chairman.
