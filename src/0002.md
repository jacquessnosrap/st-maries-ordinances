# Ordinance 2
An Ordinance to prevent the introduction and spread of contagious diseases, defining the duty of physicians and others in relation thereto, and providing for a penalty for violations of this ordinance. 
Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries.

## Section 1
Whenever any physician shall know that any person whom he is called to visit, is infected with smallpox, scarlet fever or diphtheria, or any other disease dangerous to the public health, such physician shall immediately give notice thereof to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer of this Village, and if he shall neglect or refuse to give such notice he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction shall be fined not less than TEN nor more than FIFTY DOLLARS.

## Section 2
Whenever any householder shall know that any person within his family is taken sick with the small pox, scarlet fever, diptheria or other contagious disease dangerous to the public health, he shall immediately give notice thereof to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer of this Village, and if he shall neglect or refuse to give such notice he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction shall be fined not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 3
Whenever the Chairman of the Board of Trustees of the Health Officer shall receive notice that any person within this Village is infected with small pox, scarlet fever diptheria or other contagious disease dangerous to the public health, it shall be the duty of the Health Officer to proceed to and examine such person, and if it shall be found such person is infected with any of the diseases above mentioned, it shall be the duty of the Board of Trustees under and by direction of the Health Officer to remove such sick or infected person to a separate house, if it can be done without danger to his health, and the said Board of Trustees shall make effectual provision in the manner which they shall judge best for the safety of the Inhabitants of the Village. If any such person so infected cannot be removed without danger to his health, the said Board shall take such measures as they shall deem best to isolate such person from contact with the other inhabitants of the Village, and shall post upon the house where such infected person remains a notice, on which shall be printed in letters not less than two inches in length the words, SMALL POX, or such other disease as such person may be infected which notice shall be printed upon a colored background and pasted in an conspicuous place upon such building. 

## Section 4
If any person, after the posting of the notice mentioned in the preceding section shall visit such sick or infected person without first having received the permission of the Health Officer of the physician in charge, and complying with the rule which the Board of Trustees may have adopted, he shall remain isolated, and shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and upon conviction thereof, shall be fined not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS the isolation above mentioned to continue for such time as the said Board of Trustees shall determine. 

## Section 5 
Any person bringing into this Village any clothing, bedding or other articles infected with any disease dangerous to the public health, knowing it to be so infected shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and upon conviction shall be fined not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS and it shall be the duty of the Board of Trustees, to cause such clothing bedding or other acticles to be detained and stored in some safe place such property until it shall in the opinion of the Board of Trustees, the freed from such infection provided that if in the opinion of the said Board the Storage of such property would be dangerous to the public health, there and in such case such property shall be destroyed.

Approved November 6, 1902. J W Skelton Chairman
Attest, George Hawk Clerk
