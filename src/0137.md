An ordinance impositing a license tax up on the owner or harborer or keeper of dogs;

providing for the manner of paying such tax;

for the impounding and destruction of all dogs found within the limits of the city of St Maries upon which said tax has not been paid;

providing penalties fo the violation of this ordinance;

and repealing ordinance number sixty six entitled, "An ordinance imposing a license tax upon the owner and harborer and keeper of dogs, providing for the manner of collecting such tax for the destruction of all dogs found within the limits of said village upon which said tax has not been paid, and providing penalties for the violation of this ordinance, and for allowing dogs to run at large in certain  cases; 

and repealing ordinance number twenty three entitled "An ordinance providing for the faxation of dogs in the village of St. Maries."

Be it ordained, by the mayor and the council of the city of St. Maries, Idaho:

## Section 1

It shall be unlawful for any person, owning, harboring, or keep- ing or having in his possession or under his control, any dog of either sex, to al- low the same to run at large within the corporate limits of the city of St. Maries without having first paid a license fee therefor to said city as follows, towit:- upon each female dog the eum of five dollars and upon each male dog the-sum of three dollars, which said license fee shall authorize the keeping of such dog for one year from the date thereof, and all licenses issued under this ordinance shall date from the first day of July in each year, and shall expire from the date thereof.

## Section 2

On or before the first day of July in each year such license tax shall be due and payable at the office of the city treasurer by the person so harbor- ing, owning, or keeping or having in his possession or under his control any dog of either sex. It shall be the duty of the city treasurer to receipt for such li- cense fee and upon the presentation of such receipt to the city clerk it shall be the duty of the city clerk to furnish such. party a metal check for each license issued, bearing the word "St. Maries Dog Tax" together with the number of the license and the year for which same was issued The person to whom said license was issued shall cause said check to be at- tached to a collar to be worn by the dog for which said license was issued during the time for which the license runs, and the city clerk shall keep a register of each dog for which a license is issued, with the description of the same suffic- ient for identification, the name of the dog to be entered thereon, together with the name of the person obtaining the license, the date and expiration of the license, and the sex of the dog.

## Section 3

It shall be the duty of the city police officers to sieze; kill, and. properly bury in some suitable place, all dogs found running at large in violation of this ordinance; also any and all dogs wearing collars and running at large. whose owners or keepers have failed to pay or renew the licenses Provided,- however, that no dog shall be killed un- less it has been kept in the city pound at least tour days, nor in case its owner is known, without oral or written no- tice to such owner; and provided, furth- er, that when any dog has been taken up in accordance with the provisions of this ordinance, the owner of such dog, or his representative may recover posses- ion thereof upon the payment of the li cense fee to the city treasurer, and the further fee of one dollar for each dog impounded, and the further sum of 25 cents per day for the feed and care of each dog.

## Section 4

Any person permitting any dog of which he is the owner or has the control of, to run at large within the city without having complied with all the provisions of this ordinance or shall suffer or cause a collar or number or tag to be placed or to remain on a dog, with intent to avoid payinent of license, shall upon the conviction of; such offense before any court of com- petent jurisdiction, be subject to a fine of not less than $5.00 nor more than $10.00, together with the costs of pro- secution.

## Section 5

No dog shall be impound ed which is owned harbored, or kept by any person residing out of said city of St. Maries, when such person shall have brought said dog into the city with the bona fide intention of removing said dog therefrom within forty-eight hours from the time sail dog is brought into the city.

## Section 6

Ordinance number sixty- six (66) entitled "An ordinance impos ing a license tax upon the owner and rborer or keeper of dogs; providing for the manner of collecting such taxs for the destruction of all dogs found within the limits of said village upon which said tax has not been paid; and providing for the penalties for the viola- tion of this ordinance, and for allowing dogs to run at large in certain cases," is hereby repealed.

## Section 7

Ordinance number twenty- three (23) entitled "An ordinance pro- viding for the taxation of dogs in the village of St, Maries, and every part thereof, is hereby repealed.

## Section 8

All ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict herewith are hereby repealed.

## Section 9

This ordinance shall be in full force and effect from and after its passage, approval, and publication in one issue of the St. Maries Gazette. 

Passed by the council of the city of St. Maries, this 2nd day of June 1913. 

Approved this 2nd day of June 1913. 

HENRY L. MULCAHY, Mayor of the City of St. Maries. 

Attest: CHAS. R. SCHULTE, City CLERK.
