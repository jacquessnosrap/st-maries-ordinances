AN ORDINANCE OF THE CITY OF ST. MARIES, IN KOOTENAI COUNTY, IDAHO, DECLARING DUE ADVERTISEMENT AND SALE OF $10,000 MUNICIPAL COUPON SIX PER CENT BONDS OF SAID CITY TO KEELER BROTHERS, OF DENVER, COLORADO, AS THE HIGHEST AND BEST BIDDER THEREFOR, 

AND AUTHORIZING THE ISSUANCE AND DELIVERY OF SAID BONDS TO THE PURCHASER AFORESAID,

IN ORDER TO PROVIDE FUNDS WITH WHICH TO IMPROVE CERTAIN STREETS AND AVENUES, AS AND IN THE MANNER DESIGNATED IN ORDINANCE NO. 154 OF SAID CITY,

PRESCRIBING THE FORM OF THE SAID BONDS AND THE INTEREST COUPONS TO BE ANNEXED THERETO

AND PROVIDING FOR THE LEVY, ASSESSMENT AND COLLECTION OF A DIRECT ANNUAL AD VALOREM TAX ON ALL OF THE TAXABLE PROPERTY IN SAID CITY SUF- FICIENT TO PAY THE INTEREST ACCRUING UPON SAID BONDS PROMPTLY WHEN AND AS THE SAME BECOMES DUE, AND ALSO AT THE LAWFUL TIME, A FURTHER ANNUAL AD VALORIM TAX ON SAID TAXABLE PROPERTY SUFFICIENT TO CONSTITUTE AND MAINTAIN A SINKING FUND FOR THE PAYMENT OF THE PRINCIPAL OF SAID BONDS WITHIN TWENTY YEARS FROM THE TIME OF ISSUING THE SAME, AND DECLARING AN EMERGENTY.

WHEREAS, In accordance with that certain resolution of the city council passed and approved August 28th, 1914, notice of proposal to issue and sell $10,000 municipal coupon bonds of and by the city of St. Maries, in Kootenai county, Idaho, and inviting bids therefor, was published twice each week for a period of four (4) consecutive calendar weeks in "The Record", a bi-weekly newspaper printed, published and of general circulation in said city, in the issues thereof of September 1, Spetember 4, September 8, September 11, September 15, September 18, September 22, September 25 and September 29, A. D., 1914, and evidence of such publishing has been furnished to the mayor and council of said city in the form of publisher's affidavit; and

WHEREAS, The municipal coupon bonds hereinbefore and hereinafter mentioned have heretofore, pursuant to the advertised notice of sale aforesaid, by the city treasurer, under the general supervision of the mayor and city council, been duly awarded and sold, bearing six (6) per cent interest, to Keeler Brothers, of Denver, Colorado, who were the highest and best bidders therefor, at a prine exceeding the per value of said bonds, together with all accrued interest thereon; and

WHEREAS, The total valuation of all the taxable property in said city as shown by the last assessment made in the year 1914, as finally equalized by the state board of equalization for state, county and city purposes, and as certified to the city clerk, is $752,345,00; and

WHEREAS, The total outstanding indebtedness that is a direct and general obligation of said city, of every kind and character, including all contract, bonded, warrant, floating and other indebtedness, and the $10,000 bonds by this ordinance authorized, amounts to the sum of $34,009.21 being comprised of bonds to the extent of $27,000

and warrants to the extent of $ 7,009.21 ; and

WHEREAS, The mayor and council of said city have determined and decided that all conditions, acts and things necessary and essential to the validity of the bonds of said city hereinabove and hereinafter mentioned and required by law to be done, have been fully done and per- formed; Now, Therefore, in consideration of the premises,
