# Ordinance 51

An ordinance prescribing a method for the vaction f streets, avenues, and public ways; and providing a method for the acertainment of damages caused by such vacation.

Be it ordained by the chairman and Board of trustees of the Village of St Maries:

## Section 1
That any person or body corporate owning an interest in real estate abutting on any street, avenue or public way, who may desire the vacation of such street, avenue or public way, or any such part there of as may make application therefor by causing to be filed with the city for a petition therefore, giving a description of the property to be vacated; and thereupon the board of Trustees may, and if such petition is signed by the owners of a majority of the private property abutting that part of such street, avenue or public way, shall, by resolution, at time when said petition shall be heard, which time shall be not more than forty, nor less than ten, days from teh date of the filing of such resolution; and upon the passage of such resolution it shall be duty of the petitioner to give seven days notice of the pendency of the application by a written or printed notice posted in three public places in said village, and one of which shall be posted in a conspicuous place on or adjacent to the street, avenue or public way sought to be vacated by causing such notice to be published once not less than one week to the hearing of said application, some some newspaper published in St Maries. Such notice shall contain a statement that the petition has been filed to vace the street, avenue or public way, or a portion thereof, sought to be vacted, describing the property to be vacted together with a statement of the time and place fixed for the hearin petition.

## Section 2
Any person claiming to be injured by such vation at or before the time fixed for such hearing, file his objections thereto, setting forth the grounds of such ojections, describing the property which he claims will be injured by such vacation, and setting forth the nature and extent of such injury, and the amount of damage claimed; and any person failing to so file such objections shall be deemed to have waived all claim for damages for or on account of such vacation.

## Section 3
At the time appointed for hearing such petition, or at the time to which such hearing may be adjourned, the Chairman and Board of Trustees shall consider the said petition and all objections filed thereto, and shall hear the said parties: Provided, it shall then appear to the satisfaction of said Chairman and Board of Trustees that notice of said body shall determine to grant the payer of said petition, the said Chairman and Board of Trustees shall thereupon, by ordinance, vace such street, avenue, public way, or portion thereof.

## Section 4
In The even that it shall be determed to vacate such street, avenue, public way, or portion thereof, and any claim or claims for damages by reason thereof shall have been filed as hereinbefore provided, the Chairman of said Board shall appoint three disinterested persons who shall, before the passage of such vacation ordinance, assess and determine what, if any, damages will be caused to the property of each objector; and shall immediately file with the clerk of said village, a report of their said findings, stating the amount of each item of damage.

## Section 5
This ordinance shall take effect and be in force after its passage, approval and publicationand approved by the chairman.

Passed the Board of Trustees and approved by the Chairman this 7th day of October A.D. 1907.

Penley Martin
Chairman of the Board of Trustees
