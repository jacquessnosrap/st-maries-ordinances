AN ORDINANCE VACATING A ROADWAY OR STRIP OF LAND FORTY (40) FEET IN WIDTH LYING IMMEDIATELY NORTH OF AND ADJACENT TO BLOCK TWENTY-SEVEN (27), MILWAUKEE ADDITION TO THE CITY OF ST, MARIES, BENEWAH COUNTY, IDAHO, ACCORDING TO THE RECORDED PLAT THERE OF NOW ON FILE AND OF RECORD IN THE OFFICE OF THE RECORDER OF SAID COUNTY AND STATE, AND

BOUNDED ON THE SOUTHERLY SIDE THEREOF BY THE NORTHERLY LINE OF SAID BLOCK TWENTY-SEVEN (27), ON THE NORTHERLY SIDE BY THE SOUTHERLY RIGHT-OF-WAY LINE OF THE CHICAGO, MIL- WAUKEE & ST, PAUL RAILWAY COMPANY, AS NOW THERE ESTABLISHED;

ON THE EASTERLY SIDE BY THE WESTERLY SIDE LINE OF ELEVENTH STREET EXTENDED NORTHERNLY ACROSS SAID STRIP OF LAND OR ROADWAY AND ON THE WESTERLY SIDE BY THE E STELLY SIDE LINE OF TWELFTH STREET EX- TENDED NORTHERLY ACROSS SAID STRIP OF LAND OR ROADWAY, IN THE CITY OF ST. MARIES, IDAHO, ACCORDING TO THE AFORESAID OFFICIAL PLAT THEREOF.

WHEREAS, the Chicago, Mil aukee & St. Paul Railway Company, and the Milwaukee Land Company, corporations, heretofore, to-wit: October 4th, 1915, filed with the Clerk of the City of St. Maries, Idaho, a petition for the vacation of a roadway or strip of land forty (40) feet in width, which roadway lies between Eleventh and Twelfth Streets and immeidetely North of Block Twenty-seven (27) in Milwaukee Addition to the City of St. Maries, Benewah County, Idaho, and which roadway is more particularly described herein- after, and

WHEREAS, it appearing that the aforesaid corporations were and are the owners of all of the property abutting upon the road- way or strip of land so sought to be vacated, the Mayor and City Council of the said City, by resolution duly adopted, ordered that said petition should be heard before said City Council at a meeting thereof, to be held in the City Hall in said City of St. Maries, Idaho, on the first day of November, A. D., 1915, beginning at the hour of eight o'clock P. M., and

WHEREAS, it appears to the satisfaction of the Mayor and members of the City Council of the said City that more than seven (7) days notice of the pendency of such application has been @iven by written or printed notices posted in four (4) public places in said City, one of such notices being so rosted in a conspicuous place on the said roadway sought to be vacated, and also by causing such notice to be published once, not less than one week prior to the time fixed for the hearing of said peti- tion, in a newspaper published in the City of St. Maries, Idaho, which said notices each contained a statement that a petition had been filed to vacate the strip of land or roadway described in said petition and described the property sought to be vacated and contained a statement of the time and place fixed for the hearing of said petition, and

WHEREAS, it appears that such roadway or strip of land has never been and is not now used for the purpose of public travel, nor is any other use made of said roadway by the public and that the best interests of said City of St. Maries require that said described roadway or strip of land be vacated,

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED by the Mayor and City Council of the City of St. Maries, Idaho,

## Section 1

That all of the roadway or strip of land Forty (40) feet in width, lying immediately North of and adjacent to Block Twenty-seven (27), Milwaukee Addition to the City of St. Maries, Benewah County, Idtho, according to the recorded plat thereof now on file and of reocrd in the office of the Recor- der of said County and State, ard bounded on the southerly side thereof by the northerly line of said Block Twenty-seven (27), on the northerly side by the southerly right-of-way line of the Chicago, Milwaukee & St. Paul Railway Company, as now there established; on the easterly side by the westerly side line of Eleventh Street extended northerly across said strip of land or roadway, and on the westerly side by the easterly side line of Twelfth Street extended northerly across said strip of land or roadway, in the City of St. Maries, Idaho, be, and the same is hereby, vacated.

## Section 2

This ordinance shall take effect and be in full force upon the passage, approval and publication thereof.
