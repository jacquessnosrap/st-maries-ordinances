An Ordinance authorizing the purchase of the Burial Grounds now owned by the Woodlawn Cemetery association, providing for the securing the title thereto and providing for the payment of the purchase price thereof. 

## Section 1

Whereas, a proposition has been made to the Chairman and the Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries. by the Woodlawn Cemetery Association, to assume the indebtedness of said association and take over the properties held thereby for cemetery purposes, and to assume the indebtedness of said association, amounting to the sum of about six hundred and fifty dollars ($650.00) according to a list furnished by said association, which shows an indebtedness of six hundred and fifty-one dollars and sixty-five cents ($651.65), and the said Chairman and Board of Trustees deeming it advisable to assume said indebtedness and take over the properties of said association and provide for the burial of the dead within the Village of St. Maries, 

therefore, be it ordained, by the Chairman and Board of trustees of the village of St. Maries, that they accept the proposition of said Woodlawn Cemetery Association and assume said indebtedness and take over the property now held by said association, which is described as follows, to-wit:

The NE of the SE of section 21, township 46 north, of range 2, west, Boise meridian, containing 40 acres, more or less, and that part of the N. W of the NW¼ of section 26 township 46 north, range 2 west, Boise meridian, bounded by a line commencing at the NW corner of said described land, thence south 418 feet, thence east 209 feet, thence north 418 feet and thence west 209 feet to the place of beginning, being known as the old cemetery ground of the Village of St. Maries..

## Section 2

The clerk of the said village of St. Maries is hereby authorized to draw warrants upon the treasurer of said village to pay said indebtedness, provided, however, that the price of any lots heretofore sold by the said Woodlawn Cemetery association is to be turned into said Village of St. Maries and credited upon said indebtedness as the same becomes due and payable.

## Section 3

This ordinancs shall become effective upon the delivery to the clerk of said village of a warranty deed for the property herein before described, duly signed, executed and delivered by said Woodlawn Cemetery association for the realestate hereinbefore described, and, upon the delivery of such deed the said clerk is hereby authorized to draw the warrants aforesaid, subject to the credits aforesaid.

## Section 4

That after the delivery of said deed the property shall be the property of the Village of St. Maries for the purpose aforesaid and shall be taken, held and controlled by the said Village for burial of the dead within said incorporation, under such rules and regulations as may be hereafter provided.

## Section 5

This Ordinance shall become effective upon the delivery to the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the deed aforesaid and the publication, passage and approval of this ordinance.

Approved this 1st day of April, A. D 1912.
O. E. Hailey, Chairman.
Attest Chas. R. Schulte, Clerk.
