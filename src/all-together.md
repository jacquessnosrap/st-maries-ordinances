# Ordinance 1 
An Ordinance relating to meetings of the Board of Trustees, fixing the penalty for non-attendance, and prescribing the order of business. 
Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries: 

## Section 1
The Board of Trustees until further order shall convene in the Village regularly at a place designated by, the Board on the second Monday of each month at eight P.M. Special meeting of the Board of Trustees may be called by the Chairman or any three Trustees by notice in writing from the Clerk served upon the other members of the Board. 

## Section 2 
Any member absent from a meeting of the Board without a reasonable excuse, shall be fined in the sum of one dollar for each offense. 

## Section 3
For systematic transaction of business the Chairman may apportion the members of the Board into standing committees, composed of two members each, as follows: 1st, ways and means, 2nd streets and alleys, 3rd health, 4th bills and ordinances, to which committees, all bills, resolutions, and proposed ordinances when required shall be referred for examination and consideration, and upon a favorable report of which shall be considered and disposed of in the committee of the whole by the Board of Trustees. 

## Section 4
Whenever the Board shall have convened the roll shall be called and all absentees shall be noted in the minutes of the Board and the regular order of business shall be as follows: 
### First. 
Reading, amending and approving the minutes of the previous meeting. 
### Second. 
Reports of officers. 
### Third.
Reports of committees. 
### Fourth.
Representation of petitions, remonstrances, communications and bills. 
### Fifth.
Unfinished business.
### Sixth.
New business. 

Approved November 6, 1902. J W Skelton Chairman. 
Attest. George Hoawk Clerk. 



# Ordinance 2
An Ordinance to prevent the introduction and spread of contagious diseases, defining the duty of physicians and others in relation thereto, and providing for a penalty for violations of this ordinance. 
Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries.

## Section 1
Whenever any physician shall know that any person whom he is called to visit, is infected with smallpox, scarlet fever or diphtheria, or any other disease dangerous to the public health, such physician shall immediately give notice thereof to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer of this Village, and if he shall neglect or refuse to give such notice he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction shall be fined not less than TEN nor more than FIFTY DOLLARS.

## Section 2
Whenever any householder shall know that any person within his family is taken sick with the small pox, scarlet fever, diptheria or other contagious disease dangerous to the public health, he shall immediately give notice thereof to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer of this Village, and if he shall neglect or refuse to give such notice he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction shall be fined not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 3
Whenever the Chairman of the Board of Trustees of the Health Officer shall receive notice that any person within this Village is infected with small pox, scarlet fever diptheria or other contagious disease dangerous to the public health, it shall be the duty of the Health Officer to proceed to and examine such person, and if it shall be found such person is infected with any of the diseases above mentioned, it shall be the duty of the Board of Trustees under and by direction of the Health Officer to remove such sick or infected person to a separate house, if it can be done without danger to his health, and the said Board of Trustees shall make effectual provision in the manner which they shall judge best for the safety of the Inhabitants of the Village. If any such person so infected cannot be removed without danger to his health, the said Board shall take such measures as they shall deem best to isolate such person from contact with the other inhabitants of the Village, and shall post upon the house where such infected person remains a notice, on which shall be printed in letters not less than two inches in length the words, SMALL POX, or such other disease as such person may be infected which notice shall be printed upon a colored background and pasted in an conspicuous place upon such building. 

## Section 4
If any person, after the posting of the notice mentioned in the preceding section shall visit such sick or infected person without first having received the permission of the Health Officer of the physician in charge, and complying with the rule which the Board of Trustees may have adopted, he shall remain isolated, and shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and upon conviction thereof, shall be fined not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS the isolation above mentioned to continue for such time as the said Board of Trustees shall determine. 

## Section 5 
Any person bringing into this Village any clothing, bedding or other articles infected with any disease dangerous to the public health, knowing it to be so infected shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and upon conviction shall be fined not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS and it shall be the duty of the Board of Trustees, to cause such clothing bedding or other acticles to be detained and stored in some safe place such property until it shall in the opinion of the Board of Trustees, the freed from such infection provided that if in the opinion of the said Board the Storage of such property would be dangerous to the public health, there and in such case such property shall be destroyed.

Approved November 6, 1902. J W Skelton Chairman
Attest, George Hawk Clerk



# Ordinance 3 
An Ordinance prohibiting nuisances, defining what shall be considered such, and providing for a penalty for violations of this ordinance.

Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries

## Section 1
No person or persons shall cast or leave or keep in or adjoining any street, lane, alleys square,
public place, or in any yard, lot, block or other premises within the incorporated limits of the Village of St. Maries any putrid, unsound, unwholesome or refuse meat of any animal, or any hides or skins of any kind or the whole or any part of any dead fish or animal, or any stagnant or any part of any dead fish or animal, impure water, or any unsound, putrid or unwholesome substance or the offal garbage, or the offensive pest of any animal or animals. 

## Section 2
No person or persons shall permit or suffer to accumulate in or upon any yard or premises, lot or place within the corporate limits of the Village of St, Maries, or upon any street or sidewalk adjacent to or abutting upon any lot block, place or premises occupied by his or them, or for which he or they may be agent or agents, within the Village limits, any stagnant or impure water, refuse vegetables, decayed or decaying substances, garbage or filth of any kind, nor suffer such yard, lot or premises to be to remain in such condition as to cause or create a noisome or offensive smell or atmosphere, or thereby to be or become cause or create, a public nuisance. 

## Section 3
No person who is the owner or occupant of or agent for any house, store, building or premises, in the Village of St. Maries, to which a privy belongs or appertains shall use or keep the same in such a condition, as to cause a noisome or offensive smell so as to become a nuisance. 

## Section 4
Whenever the Health Officer shall be informed of the existance of any nuisance prohibited by the preceding sections, he shall immediately make or cause to be made an examination of the yard, lot, place or premises on which it is charged such nuisance is suffered or permitted and if a misance is found to exist thereon, or to exist in or upon any street, lane, alley or public place, or in or upon any gutter, or sidewalk adjacent to or abutting upon said vard, lot, place or premises, he shall forthwith give or cause to be given, either to owner, agent or occupant of such vard, lot, place or premises, or all of them, a notice to abate or remove said muisance, and if such nuisance is not abated or removed within twenty four hours after such notice the marshall shall cause the owner, occupant of or agent of such the property to be arrested and brought before the proper court, and if he or they be convicted, the marshall shall cause the same to be removed, and he shall be and is hereby authorized to remove any constructions that it may be necessary to remove in order to reach and remove and abate such nuisance,the expense of removing any nuisance by the marshal shall be paid by the Village in the first instance, but the same shall be taxed as a part of the costs in such cause, and recovered by the Village from the owner, agent or occupant notified as hereinbefore provided. 

## Section 5
If any person or persons shall violate any of the provisions of this ordinance, they shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof, shall be fined not less than five dollars nor more than fifty dollars, or imprisonment not less than two nor more than twenty days and in all cases of conviction the costs of the case together with the costs of abating or removing the nuisance shall be taxed against the defendant.

Approved November 6, 1902. 
J W Skelton Chairman.
Attest. George Hawk Clerk




# Ordinance 4
An Ordinance restraining cattle running at large and prescribing penalty for its violation. 

Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries: 

## Section 1
That no horses, mules, cattle sheep or hogs shall be allowed to run at large in any of the streets, lanes, or alleys of the Village of St. Maries, during the hours between seven o'clock P.M. and six o'clock A.M All animals so running at large may be taken up and the Village Marshal and impoundedin a pound or inclosure to be provided by the Board of Trustees, and such animals shall be provided with proper care, food and water while so confined. 

## Section 2
The Village Marshal shall give immediate notice of the taking up of such animals with a discription thereof by posting the same in three conspicuous places in this Village. 

## Section 3
If any of the animals mentioned above be taken up within the limits of the Village of Saint Maries at the expiration of five days after the giving of the official notice, the Village Marshal shall sell such animal at public auction, and after deducting the cost of keeping and selling, he shall pay the remainder of such proceeds into the village Treasury to be paid to the owner of such animal upon demand. # Ordinance 5

An Ordinance providing for the prevention of fires and the protection of person and property endangered thereby, and providing penalties for the violation thereof. 
Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries. 

## Section 1
That all buildings now located, or that may hereafter may be placed or built within the limits of this villiage, and at a less distance than one hundred feefrom any other building, or any building that may be brought within that distance by the erection of other buildings, shall be provided with chimneys whose walls shall be of a good quality of brick at least four inches in thickness, and laid in good lime mortar and to be plastered on the inside.
Provided, that buildings already erected, and which have chimneys, of concrete, terra cotta, or so called patent chimneys, constructed of two iron pipes, of sheet iron, one within the other with at least two inches of air space between them, every joint of said pipe being riveted together, and the two pipes joined at each end, and holes provided for a free circulation of air between the two pipes, said chimneys being in good, good and safe condition, shall be deemed to have complied with this ordinance but all new chimneys shall be built of brick as aforesaid. 

## Section 2
No stove pipe in any building with wooden or combustable floors and ceilings shall enter any flue unless said pipe shall be at least sixteen inches from either floor or ceilings, or be fully protected by tin or zinc. And in all cases where smoke pipes or stove pipes pass through wooden floors or partitions of any kind, whether the same be plastered or not, they shall be guarded by a double collar of metal, with at least four inches of air space, between, and holes for ventilation, or by a solid coating of mortar or plaster of paris three inches thick, or by an earthern ware piping three inches from the pipe.

## Section 3
No stovepipe or smoke pipe shall pass through any window, side or roof of any building, except temporarily by permit in writing, granted by the firewarden, and such permit shall designate the time for which leave is granted. The opening where the pipe goes through the roof or side of the building shall in all cases be at least four inches in diameter larger than the size of the pipe, and be protected by a double collar of metal or otherwise, as in case of passing through partitions, and tin or zinc on the outside and inside of such openings. 

## Section 4
All chimneys shall be well secured, and shall extend at least three feet above the roof of the building to which they are attached, measuring from the point where the chimney or flue be deemed unsafe to the building, or adjoining buildings by the fire warden they shall be carried up to such a height as may be deemed necessary not to exceed three feet above the extreme height of said building or the buildings adjoining. 

## Section 5
That any flue, smoke pipe or chimney which shall be deemed by the fire warden to be unsafe, the fire warden shall notify the owner or occupant of the building that the same must be repaired or removed within ten days, and if the  parties notified shall presist in using any flue, smokepipe or chimney which endangers the building to which it is attached or adjoining buildings the fire warden shall cause the same to be repaired or removed, and the costs of such repairs or removal shall be made at the expense of the owner or occupant of the building 

## Section 6 
That the Village Marshall, shall until a regular fire warden be appointed, act for and be the fire warden 
of this Village.

## Section 7
That the fire warden of this Village shall at all times have the right to enter any building for the purpose of discovering or ascertaining any violation of this ordinance.

## Section 8 
That it shall be the duty of the fire warden to make an inspection of all buildings now in this Village, 
and ascertain if the chimneys, smoke and stove pipes and constructed are placed in accordance with the provisions of this ordinance. 

## Section 9 
A violation of any of the provisions of this ordinance shall be deemed a misdemenor and punished by a fine of not less than FIVE and not more than TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS, OR by imprisonment for not less than five more than twenty five days or both fine and imprisonment, at the discretion of the court, and the continuance or maintaining of such violation shall be deemed a new offense for each day on which the same is so continued or maintained, and shall be punished accordingly.

Approved November 6, 1902.
Attest. George Hawk Clerk.

J W Skelton Chairman.



# Ordinance 6

An Ordinance prohibiting, disorderly conduct, defining what shall be considered such, and providing for penalties for violation thereof.

Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Villages of St Maries

## Section 1
That any person or persons, who shall be guilty of any violent riotous or disorder by conduct, or who shall use any profane, abusive or obsene language, in any street, house or place, whereby the peace or quiet of the village is or may be disturbed, or who shall in public, be guilty of any indecent or immoral act or practice in said Village or shall be found in a helpless condition upon any street or public place, in this Village through intoxication, shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and shall upon conviction before any competent court be fined not less than FIVE nor more than TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS, or be imprisoned in the Village jail not less than five nor more than twenty five days, or both fine and imprisonment, at the discretion of the court. 

## Section 2
That any person or persons who, shall fire off or discharge any ordinance, gun, pistol, rifle, or other 
firearm within the corporate limits of the Village shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof before any justice of the peace, having jurisdiction shall be fined, not to exceed FIFTY DOLLARS provided, that upon national holidays and other days of public celebration the Board of Trustees may permit under such regulations as they deem bestaria , the firing of such ordinance, and other firearms. Provided further that the Board of Trustees may issue permits for the running of any shooting gallery, if they deem best for public interests.

## Section 3
That any person or persons who shall be guilty of any assault, or of any battery, within the limits of the Village shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof, shall be fined not less than ONE nor more than FIFTY DOLLARS, or by imprisonment not less than one nor more than fifty days, or by both fine and imprisonment, at the discretion of the court.

## Section 4
Any person or persons who shall draw any species of a firearm, or any dirk, dagger, or knife, or other deadly or dangerous weapon in threatening, menacing or insolent manner, with the intent, of using the same upon the person of another, or threatening to so use said weapon, or in a manner which shall cause any person to fear that it may be used upon his person, such person shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and upon conviction there of shall be fined not less than TWENTY FIVE and not to exceed ONE HUNDRED DOLLARS.

## Section 5
Any person or persons who shall resist any peace officer, or shall refuse to assist him in the discharge of his duties, or who shall by any means aid or assist any person in custody upon a charge of violating any of the Ordinances of the Village in his endeavors to escape from such custody, whether such escape be effected or not, shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof to shall be fined not less than ONE and not more than FIFTY DOLLARS, or by imprisonment in the Village jail not lass than one day, nor more than fifty days or by both fine and imprisonment, at the, discretion of the court. 

## Section 6
Any person who shall throw or deposit in any street, sidewalk or footpath of this Village any broken glass bottles, crockery, nails or other substance whatever whereby the feet of horses, pedestrians or any beast of burden, may be injured or shall throw, deposit of sweep into or upon any street sidewalk or footpath of the Village any paper or other substance whatever except snow or dirt resulting from travel shall be deemed guilt of a misdemenor and upon conviction thereof shall be fined not less than ONE and not more than TEN DOLLARS.

## Section 7
Any person who shall cruelly beat, torture, misuse, deprive of food and water or otherwise treat any animal with cruelty within this corporation limit of the Villages shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof shall be fined not less than five nor more than twenty five dollars. 

## Section 8
If any person or persons shall introduce or take into, the Village jail or deliver to any prisoner confined therein, any wine spiritous or malt liquors, except by permission of the marshal or upon the prescription of a practicing physician he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor, and upon conviction thereof shall be fined not less than FIVE nor more than TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS or by imprisonment, or by both fine and imprisonment at the discretion of the court.

## Section 9 
Any person under the age of sixteen who goes into or frequents at any time any saloon or place where liquor is sold, or into any gambling house except by the permission of the parent or guardian of such child, or for any person to entice or persuade any such child to enter or frequent auch place or places, any person found violating the provisions of the section shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and on conviction thereof it be punished by fine in any sum not exceeding fifty dollars or by imprisonment in the city jail not exceeding ten days, or by both such fine and imprisonment at the discretion of the court.

## Section 10
No bawdy house, house of ill-fame or disorderly house shall be kept or maintained within this city; and every person who shall set up, open or keep such house, or shall be an inmate of or in any way connected with such house, and every person who shall permit any tenement, building or premises in his or her possession, or under his or her control to be used as any such house shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor and upon conviction thereof, shall be punished by a fine not less than five dollars or more than fifty dollars, or by imprisonment not less than five days nor more than twenty days, or by both such fine and imprisonment for each offense and it shall be the duty of the Village Marshal and his deputies, and all police officers and night watchmen of the Village, either upon their knowledge, information or belief, or upon the complaint of any citizen, to arrest and take before a Magistrate for trial any person who shall violate any of the provisions of this section, and any such officers who having knowledge of the violation of any of the provisions of this section, shall neglect or fail so to do, shall be deemed guilty of a misdemenor in office, and upon conviction of such neglect or failure, shall be puhished by a fined of not less than five nor more than fifty dollars. 
In all cases of prosecutions under Section 10 of this ordinance, common fame shall be competent evidence in supporting of the complaint, and every house or place used or occupied for purposes of prostitution, fornication, assignation or lewdness, shall be taken and deemed to be a bawdy house within the meaning of this ordinance, in any prosecution against any person or persons for violating any of the provisions of this ordinance. 

## Section 11
In all cases of conviction for any of the offenses mentioned in this ordinance the court shall adjudge the offender to pay the costs of the prosecution and shall in default of the payment of both fine and costs, commit the offender to the Village jail for a term of imprisonment not exceeding ninety days, and in all cases when the term of imprisonment in the Village jail in lieu of fine, the offender shall be imprisoned in default of payment of fine, one day for every two dollars of such fine.

# Ordinance 7 

An Ordinance providing for the taxation of dogs. 

Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries.

## Section 1
That the owner or person having in charge any dog permitted to run at large within the corporate limits of this Village, shall on or before the fifteenth day of November of each year pay into the Village Treasury the sum of three dollars for each male dog and the sum of ten dollars for each female dog, which shall entitle him to a receipt from the Treasurer, designating the owners name, and the number of the license, which number shall correspond with that worn by the dog, provided that for the year. 

## Section 2
That no dog shall be permitted to run at large in any of the streets, squares, lanes, alleys or public places of the Village, without having a collar of leather or metal secured around his neck with the number of his license stamped or engraved thereon. 

## Section 3
The Treasurer shall keep a suitable record of the licenses granted and on the expiration of the annual time for issuing license shall give the Village Marshall a list of the numbers on which licenses have been paid or received, and of the numbers which have not been renewed, and the names of the owners or representatives of the dogs having such renewment numbers. 

## Section 4
The term for which licenses shall be granted shall be from the first Tuesday of the fiscal year in which the license is granted until the First Tuesday of May of the succeeding year. 

## Section 5
It shall be the duty of the Village Marshall, to seize, kill, and properly bury in some suitable place all dogs found running at large in violation of section two of this ordinance, also any and all dogs wearing collars, on which the owners or representatives have failed to pay or renew the licenses, provided that when a dog wearing a collar has been taken up, the Marshall shall notify the owner or representative thereof, who may thereupon recover possession, upon payment of the license to the Treasurer, and a fee of fifty cents to the Marshall for each male dog and a fee of one dollar for each female dog, provided further, that no dog shall be killed unless it has been kept in the Village Pound at least forty eight hours. 

## Section 6
Any person or inhabitant of this Village permitting any dog of which he is the owner, or over which he has control, to go abroad in any of the streets squares, lanes, alleys or public places of this Village without having first paid the license requiredby this ordinance, and having the number thereof stamped or engraved upon a collar upon such dogs hereinbefore provided, or who shall suffer or cause a collar or number to be placed or remain, with intent to avoid payment of license shall upon conviction of such offense, before any court of competent jurisdiction be subject to a fine of not less than FIVE nor more than TEN DOLLARS. 

## Section 7
It shall be the duty of the Village Marshall to prosecute all violations of this ordinance.

## Section 8
No license shall issue for less tha one year provided, that for the present year, that licenses may issue to be paid pro rata, for the unexpired portion of the first year ending, the first Tuesday of May of the year 1903.

# Ordinance 10

An Ordinance to prevent the introduction and spread of contagious diseases defining the duties of physicians and others in relation thereto, and providing penalties for violations thereof.

Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries, Kootenai County, State of Idaho.

## Section 1
Whenever any physician shall know that any person whom he is called to visit, is infected with small pox, scarlet fever or diptheria, or other disease dangerous to public health such physician shall immediately give notice thereof to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer of this Village, and if he shall neglect or refuse to give such notice, he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction thereof before any court of competent jurisdiction shall be fined in any sum not less than ten nor more than fifty dollars.

## Section 2
Whenever any person shall know that any person within his family is taken sick with smallpox scarlet fever or deptheris or any other contagious disease dangerous to public health, he shall immediately give notice thereof to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer of this Village, and if he neglect or refuse to give such notice He shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction thereof before any court of competent jurisdiction be fined in any sum not to exceed the sum of TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 3
Whenever the chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer shall receive notice that any person within this Village is infected with small pox, scarlet fever or diptheria, or other disease dangerous to public health, it shall be the duty of the health officer to proceed to and examine such person, and for that purpose, if there be any doubt as to the nature of the disease, employ, with assent of the Chairman of the Board of Trustees, a competent physician to assist in such examination, the expanse of which shall be audited and paid as are other charges against the Village, If it is found after such examination that such person is infected with any of the diseases above mentioned or any other disease dangerous to the public health, it shall be the duty of the health officer to post upon the house where such infected person resides or remains a notice, on which shall be printed in letters not less than two inches in length, the words, "SMALL POX" "SCARLET FEVER", or such other disease as such person may be infected, and shall also in the case of small pox place a yellow flag in some conspicuous place on said premises, and in the case of scarlet fever a scarlet or red flag, and in other cases a white flag, and the Board of Trustees shall make effectual provision in the manner which they shall judge best for the safety of the inhabitants of the Village, such notice and flag shall remain upon said premises until such person having such disease shall in the judgement of the health officer be so far recovered that no danger from infection remains, and such premises have been cleaned, purified and disinfected to the satisfaction of the said health officer, and it shall be his duty to see that they are so cleansed, purified and disinfected.

Any person removing such notice or flag placed upon such premises without the written consent of said health officer shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction shall be fined in any sum not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 4
All persons who are upon the premises where such sick and infected person remains either as occupants, residents or there for temporary purposes, shall after the posting of the notice provided for in the preceding section remain on said premises unless permission be given by the health officer to remove therefrom and any such person who shall be found upon any public street or in any place where he may come in contact or mingle with people not affected with the disease with which such sick person is infected, shall be fined in any sum not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 5
Any person who after the posting of the notice mentioned in Section 3 of this ordinance shall visit such sick person or shall go into any building where such notice is erected without first having obtained permission in writing of the health officer and then only upon conditions which he shall impose, he shall remain upon such premises until such time as permission in writing is given by such health officer to remove therefrom and he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be fined in any sum not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 6
Any person bringing into this Village any clothing, bedding of other articles infected with any disease dangerous to public health, knowing it to be so infected, shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be fined not to exceed twenty five dollars, and it shall be the duty of the Board of Trustees to cause such clothing, bedding of other articles to be detained and stored in some safe place until in the opinion of the Board it be freed from such infection, provided, that if in the opinion of the said Board the storage of such property would be dangerous to the public health, then and in such case such property should be destroyed.

Approved November 6, 1902.

That there be and is appropriated out of all monies received from licenses during the fiscal year ending the first Tuesday in May, 1908, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $400.00 to the Salary Fund, to be used for the purpose of paying the salaries of the various village officers.

## Section 3
That there be and hereby is appropriated out of the money received from taxation, and collected for licenses, fines, impounded animals and dog taxes, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $500.00 to the Street Fund, to be used for the purpose of improving, maintaining and repairing the streets in said village.

## Section 4
That there be and hereby is appropriated out of all monies received from taxation and for licenses, fines, impounding animals and dog taxes, during the fiscal year ending the first Tuesday in May, 1908, the sum of $800.00 to the Sinking Fund, to be used for the purpose of paying the interest on the outstanding bonded indebtedness and also for the purpose of creating a fund with which to pay the principal when due.

## Section 5
That there be and hereby is appropriated out of money received from taxation, and collected for licenses, fines, impounding animals and dog taxes during the fiscal year ending the first Tuesday in May, 1908, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $100.00 to the Dock Fund, to be used for the purpose of repairing and maintaining the docks in said village.

## Section 6
That there be and hereby is appropriated from money received from taxation, and collected for licenses, fines, impounding animals and dog taxes during the fiscal year ending the first Tuesday in May, 1908, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $350.00 to the Incidental Fund, to be used for the purpose of paying the incidental expenses of said village.

## Section 7
That there be and hereby is appropriated from money received from taxation, and collected for licenses, fines and water rentals and charges during the fiscal year ending the first Tuesday in May, 1908, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $1000.00 to Water Fund, to be used in operating, improving and maintaining the water system of said village.

## Section 8
That in accordance with the provisions of this ordinance it shall be the duty of the Village Treasurer to apportion all of the money received by him to the various funds as herein mentioned, and to keep a separate account of each fund.

## Section 9
That this ordinance shall take effect and be in force after its passage, approval and publication.

PASSED by the Board and approved by the Chairman this 10th day of July, 1907.

Attest: Thomas White, Village Clerk.

Joseph Fisher,
Chairman



# ORDINANCE NO. 46

An ordinance fixing and establishing a city base, and providing that the official grades and elevations of all streets and avenues shall be fixed and established with reference to and computed therefrom.

BE IT ORDAINED BY THE CHAIRMAN AND BOARD OF TRUSTEES OF THE VILLAGE OF ST. MARIES, IDAHO:

## SECTION 1

That a city base be and hereby is fixed and established at a point at the northwest corner of the intersection of First Street and College Avenue, the said point being 2148.30 feet above sea level, and marked by an iron bar.

## SECTION 2

That all official grades and elevations hereafter fixed and established on any of the Streets and Avenues in the Village of St. Maries, Idaho, shall be with reference to and computed from the city base as fixed and established in Section 1 of this ordinance.

## SECTION 3

That this ordinance shall take effect and be in force after its passage, approval and publication.

PASSED and APPROVED this 5th day of August, 1907.

Joseph Fisher, Chairman.

# Introduction to the Ordinances of the City of Saint Maries
