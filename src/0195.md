AN ORDINANCE OF THE CITY OF ST. MARIES REGULATING TRAFFIC UPON THE STREETS AND AVENUES AND OTHER PLACES WITHIN THE CORPORATE LIMITS OF THE CITY OF ST. MARIES, IDAHO;

MAKING IT UNLAWFUL TO DRIVE ANY AUTOMOBILE, MOTOR VEHICLE OR MOTOR CYCLE UPON ANY OF THE STREETS OR AVENUES OR WITHIN THE CORPORATE LIMITS UNLESS THE SAME IS EQUIPPED AND DRIVEN AS PROVIDED IN SAID ORDINANCE;

PROVIDING THAT ANY SUCH VEHICLE SHALL BE LICENSED, EQUIPPED WITH BRAKES AND HORN AND WITH LIGHTS;

PROVIDING MEANS AND HETHOD OF STOPPING AUTOMOBILE, MOTOR VEHICLE OF MOTOR CYCLE;

PROVIDING MEANS OF PASSING WHEN GOING IN THE SATE OR OPPOSITE DIRECTIONS;

RPOVIDING A SAFE METHOD FOR OPERATING ANY AUTOMOBILE, MOTOR VE ICLE OR MOTOR CYCLE AND WHO SHALL OPERATE OR DRIVE AUTOMOBILE, MOTOR VEHILE OR MOTOR CУСТЕ;

PROVIDING A PLACE WHERE AUTOMOBILES, MOTOR VEHICLES OR MOTOR CYCLES AND OTHER MEANS OF TRAVEL MAY STOP UPON THE STREETS OR AVENUES OF THE CITY OF ST. MARIES;

PROVIDING WHICH AUTOMOBILE, MOTOR VEHICLE OR MOTOR CYCLE SHALL HAVE THE RIGHT OF WAY, FIXING A MAXIMUM SPEED LIMIT UPON ITS STREETS AND AVENUES;

PROVIDING THAT DRIVERS OF SUTOMOBILES, MOTOR VEHICLES OR MOTOR CYCLES, SHALL STOP THEN COLLANDED BY ANY POLICEMAN, SHERIFF OR DEPUTY SHERIFF;

PROVIDING AND FIXING A PENALTY FOR THE VIOLATION OF SUCH ORDINANCE; REPLALING ALL ORDINANCES IN FONELICT HRAWITH AND FOR OTHER PUPPOSES.

BE IT ORDAINED by the Mayor and City Council of the City of St. Maries, Benewah County, Idaho:-

## Section 1.

It shall be and is unlawfull for any person or persons, upon any of the streets or avenues of the City of St. Maries, Idaho, to operate, run or drive any automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle except as in this ordinance provided.

## Section 2.

No person or persons shall operate an automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle on any of the streets or avenues in the City of St. Maries, unless said automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle shall have been licensed by the State of Idaho, or some other state in the manner provided by law and shall have a number plate exhibited in a conspicious place upon said automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle, showing that said automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle has been licensed and that said license is in force and effect at the time said automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle is being operated on said streets or avenues.

## Section 3.

Every automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle, driven or operated upon any of the streets or avenues of the City of St. Maries, shall be provided with adequate brakes, in good working order and sufficinet to control said vehicle at all times when the same is in use, and further, shall be equipped with a horn or other device for signaling under all conditions so as to give quick and timely warning of the approach of aaid automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle, and shall further, during the period subsequent to one hour after sunset and one hour prior or before sunrise, display at least two lighted lamps on the front and one on the rear of said automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle. The light of the front lamps shall be visible at least two hundred feet in the direction in which the automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle is proceeding.

## Section 4.

Every person operating or driving an automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle on the public streets or avenues within the corporate limits of the City of St. Maries, Idaho, shall when crossing intersecting streets or avenues, or turning any corner on any street or avenue, slow down the speed of the same, and shall when turning corners sound his horn or other device for signaling in such a manner as to give notice and warning of his approach; provided, that motor cycles shall be considered to have complied with the provisions of this section relative to lights by carrying one front lamp visible for a distance of one hundred feet in the direction it is proceeding.

## Section 5.

Whenever any person or persons operating any automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle shall meet on the streets or avenues of the City of St. Maries or within its corporate limits, any other person riding or iriving a horse or horses or draft animals of any description, or any other means of cenveyance or other motor vehicle, automobile, or motor cycle, the person se meeting shall turn to the right so as to give at least half of the street or avenue for such other person to pass; if they are traveling in the same direction the person over-taking and passing such other person shall pass to the left anithe person over-taken shall give the over-taking vehicle a convenient opportunity so to pass to his left.

## Section 6.

All persons driving or riding any automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle, rig, buggy, wagon, buck board or other means of travel around any corner of the streets or avenues of the City of St. Maries, Idaho, and when and while turning corners shall drive such automobile, motor vehicle, motor cycle, rig, buggy, wagon, buck board or other means of conveyance to the right of the center of the inter-secting streets and avenues. When turning around on any of the inter-sections of the avenues or streets off the City of St. Maries, all such means of conveyance as described in this section shall be driven to the right of the center of the inter-section of the streets and avenues.

## Section 7.

Every person driving or operating an automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle on the streets or avenues of the City of St. Maries, Idaho, shall drive the same in a careful manner, at a rate of speed so as not to endanger the property of another or the life or limb of any person.

## Section 8.

No person or persons shall operate any automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle upon any of the streets or avenues or highways of the City of St. Maries when intoxicated.

## Section 9.

No automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle shall De turned around on any of the streets ar avenues in the City of St. Maries, except at the inter-section of the streets and avenues and while turning around such automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle shall be driven to the right of the center of said inter-section of streets or avenues.

## Section 10.

No automobile, motor vehicle, motor cycle, or any rig, wagon, buggy, buck board or any other means of travel or conveyance shall be stopped on any of the streets or left standing on any of the streets of the City of St. Maries for any period of time except as follows: All such automobiles, motor vehicles, motor cycles rigs, wapons, bugries or other means of conveyance must be stopped only on the right side of the street from the direction in which such automobile, motor vehicle, motor cycle, rig, wagon, buggy or other means of conveyance are proceeding. Means of conveyance herein described going north will stop only on the east side of the street, those going south will stop only on the west side of the street, those going west will stop only on the north side of the street and those going east will stop only on the south side of the street.

## Section 11.

Automobiles, motor vehicles or motor cycles running east or west on any of the avenues of the City of St. Maries shall be deemed to have the right of way. All persons driving north or south on any of the avenues of the City of St. Maries shall permit automobiles, motor vehicles or motor cyeles runding east or west to proceed without interference, and in case of any danger of collision the automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle shall stop till the one going east or west shall pass.

## Section 12.

No automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle shall be driven on any of the streets or avenues of the City of St. Maries at any time at a rate of speed exceeding 15 miles per hour. No automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle shall be driven across any crossing, intersection of avenues and streets at a rate of speed exceeding 12 miles per hour. No automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle shall be driven around any corner at a rate of speed exceeding 8 miles per hour.

## Section 13.

All persons riding or driving any automobile motor vehicle or motor cycle upon any of the improved streets or avenues of the City of St. Maries, shall where practicable ride or drive such automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle, to the right of the center line of the street or avenue on which he is so riding or driving.

## Section 14.

No person under sixteen (16) years of age shall under any circumstances operate or drive any automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle upon any of the streets or avenues of the City of St. Maries. No person not thoroughly familiar with the automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle which he or she is driving who is fully competent to drive the same shall shall operate or drive any automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle on any of the streets or avenues of the City of St. Maries under any circumstances; provided, however, that the above shall not apply to a person accompanied by a chaueffeur, qualified, and competent, who is being instructed in driving or operating such automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle.

## Section 15.

Any person riding or driving any automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle, when turning any corner or turning around on any inter-section or streets or avenues, or at any time shall indicate by motion of his hand and by pointing the way he intends to turn or go.

## Section 16.

No automobile, motor vehicle, motor cycle, or other vehicle shall be left standing on any of the streets or avenues within the City of St. Maries with the engine left running, and shall not be parked or left standing, whether occupied or unoccupied, within any street intersection or across any crossing.

## Section 17.

Any person violating any of the provisions of this ordinance shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and upón conviction shall be fined not less than $5.00 and not to exceed $100.00, or may be imprisoned not less than one day and not to exceed ten days, or may be punished by both such fines and imprisonment in the discretion of the court and in addition shall, in all cases, pay the costs of prosecution.

## Section 18.

Any person driving any automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle within the corporate limits of the City of St. Maries, must stop sich automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle at any time or at any place when commanded by any policeman of St. Maries, or any sheriff or deputy sheriff of Benewah County to do so. Any person not stopping any such automobile, motor vehicle or motor cycle when commanded by any policeman of the City of St. Maries, or sheriff or deputy sheriff, shall be guilty of a misdeameanor and punished as provided in Section 17 of this Ordinance.

All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict herewith are hereby repealed.

This ordinance shall take effect and be in full force from and after its approval and publication in one issue of the St. Maries Gazette a newspaper published in the City of St. Maries, Idaho.
