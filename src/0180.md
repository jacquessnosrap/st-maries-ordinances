AN ORDINANCE OF THE CITY OF ST. MARIES, IDAHO, DIRECTING AND AUTHORIZING THE MAYOR AND CITY CLERK OF SAID CITY TO BORROW A SUFFICIENT AMOUNT OF MONEY TO PAY A CERTAIN JUDGMENT IN FAVOR OF ANNIE BEATON AND JOHN BEATON AGAINST SAID CITY OF ST. MARIES, WHICH SAID JUDGMENT AMOUNTS TO THE SUM OF $2097.93, INCLUDING INTEREST AND COSTS TO DATE AND PROVIDING THAT A TAX SHALL BE LEVIED AGAINST ALL TAXABLE PROPERTY OF THE CITY OF ST. MARIES FOR THE YEAR 1916, FOR THE PURPOSE OF PAYING SAID LOAN.

BE IT ORDAINED by the Mayor and City Council of the City of St. Maries, Idaho:

THAT WHEREAS, Annie Beaton and John Beaton, her husband, procured a judgment in the District Court of the Eighth Judicial District of the State of Idaho, for Kootenai County, against the City of St. Maries, amounting to the principal sum of $1800. and costs amounting to $139.55, said judgment having been entered as of date, December 12, 1914, and which said total amount and judgment bears interest at 7% from December 12, 1914, and which said judgment, interest and costs are unsatisfied.

AND WHEREAS, The said City is without sufficient funds with which to pay said judgment and liability.

THEREFORE

## Section 1.

The Mayor and City Clerk of said City of St. Maries are hereby empowered, authorized and directed to borrow on behalf of said city, from such person, bank or corporation as may to them seem fit and advisable, an amount equal to said judgment, together with costs and interest to the date of settling the same, said loan not to exceed $2100.00, and to pledge the faith and credit of the said City of St. Maries for the re-payment of said loan, together with interest at the rate of 6% per annum from the date of borrowing said sum until paid. And the said Mayor and Clerk are further authorized, empowered and directed to execute evidence of said indebtedness, together with a promise to repay the same in the name and on behalf of said City of St. Maries. That said loan shall be repaid and said note or bond or other evidence of indebtedness executed in the name of said City of St. Maries by the Mayor and Clerk of said City shall be made payable on or before the close of the next fiscal year, and shall draw interest at 6% per annum from its date until paid, interest payable semi-annually.

## Section 2.

That the faith and credit of the City of St. Maries is hereby pledged for the payment of the said principal and interest of said note or bond, or evidence of indebtedness when the same becomes due, and the City Council of said City of St. Maries is hereby authorized and directed to provide a fund for the payment of the same at the time the annual appropriation ordinance for the fiscal year beginning in the year 1916 is enacted. And the said City Council is further ordered, authorized and directed to add the amount of said loan and interest to the amount authorized to be raised in the general tax levy for the fiscal year beginning in 1916.

## Section 3.

That all ordinances and parts of ordinances inconsistent with this ordinance are hereby repealed and this ordinance shall take effect immediately upon its passage, approval and publication.

The above ordinance was duly and regularly enacted an ordinance of the City of St. Maries under suspension of rules requiring an ordinance to be read for three separate days.

Passed and approved this 7th day of February, 1916.

Attest:
S. T. Schulte, City Clerk.
O. E. Hailey, Mayor.

(Published in the St. Maries Gazette, February 11, 1916)
