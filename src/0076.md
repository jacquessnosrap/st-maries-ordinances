An ordinance creating Local Sewerage District No. 1 in the Village of St. Maries, Kootenai County, Idaho, defining the boundaries thereof and providing for the construction of sewerage system in said District; providing for the appointment of a sewerage committee and fixing their powers and duties; providing for a special assessment upon the property within said district for said improvement and expenses connected therewith and for the issuance of bonds therefor; providing for the payment of said assessment in ten (10) equal annual installments and for the collection thereof; providing the duties of the Village Clerk and Treasurer in connection with the collection and providing for the redemption of said bonds; providing for the assessment upon the property of said District and the hearing of protests against the same.

Be it ordained by the chairman and board of trustees of the Village of St. Maries, Kootenai County, Idaho:

## Sec. 1

There is hereby established "Local Sewerage Improvement District No. 1" in the Village of St. Maries, including all the lots or parcels of land included within the boundaries lines to-wit: Commencing at a point where the center line of the alley between Front and College Avenues intersects the center line of Eighth Street; thence north along the center line thereof to where same intersects the South line of the Chicago Milwaukee and St Paul Railway Company Right of way; thence with the same to an intersection with the center line of Idaho Avenue; thence west along the center line of said Avenue to an intersection with the center line of Tenth Street; thence north along said line to the St. Joe River; thence in a northwesterly direction with the meander line of said River to an intersection with the Boundary line of the Coeur d'Alene Indian Reservation; thence with said line in a southwesterly direction to an intersection with the center line of Eleventh Street; thence along said line to the center line of the alley aforesaid between College and Front Avenues; thence along said center line to place of beginning, save and except the property occupied by the streets cross streets and alleys therein. The main trunk or outlet beginning at a point near the intersection of the center line of Eleventh Street with the center line of the Alley between College and Front Avenues and running along same to a point near the Reservation line aforesaid: thence across lot one (1) of Block Three (3) of Railroad Addition to the Village of St. Maries to and emptying into the St. Joe River near where the said reservation line intersects the meander line of said River.

## II.

The general character of said proposed sewer system shall be as follows to-wit: A gravity system, the laterals and mains of which are to be placed within the streets and Avenues within said District. The pipe for same to be Vitrified Sewer Pipe and mains and laterals to be laid as follows, to-wit: The main trunk to be laid from the alley mentioned along Eleventh Street to a point near the Reservation line and across lot one (1) as aforesaid to the St. Joe River shall be of 12 inch pipe; laterals to be laid between Eighth and Eleventh Streets on Front Avenue and between Eighth and Eleventh Streets on Center Avenue shall be of 8 inch pipe; the lateral to be laid between Eighth and Eleventh Streets on Idaho Avenue to be of 6 inch Pipe; the location and sizes of pipe herein given are for the purpose of stating the general character of said proposed system and are subject to any necessary alteration in the construction of said System.

## III.

The estimated costs of said system is the sum of $ 7000.00 and to conform to plans and specifications to be prepared by the Village engineer and the cost thereof not to exceed the estimate aforesaid.

## IV.

There is hereby provided for a committee to be known as the "Sewer Committee" of which there shall be three members, each a bona fide resident and substantial taxpayer of said Village of St. Maries. Members of said committee shall be appointed by the Chairman of the Board of Trustees as provided by law, one member for one year, one for two years and the third for three years and they shall hold their office until their successors are elected and qualified, and shall receive as compensation the sum of \[blank\] dollars for each day actually employed in such labor.

## V.

The said Committee shall organize by the election of a president who shall preside over its meetings and who shall hold his office at the pleasure of the committee; they shall also elect the Village clerk as clerk of said committee and shall elect a treasurer who shall be the Village treasurer who shall give an additional bond over and above his bond as Village treasurer in the sum of one thousand Dollars.

## VI.

The president or Chairman of said committee shall execute all necessary contracts on behalf of said committee and shall sign all orders for the payment of money authorized by said committee to be paid.

## VII.

The clerk of said committee shall keep minutes of its meeting, countersign all orders authorized by it and keep a proper account Book showing all matters of account in connection with the business of said Committee and have custody of all its papers.

## VIII.

The Treasurer of said committee shall have charge of all money coming into the hands of the committee and shall pay out the same upon proper vouchers therefor.

## IX.

The construction of said sewerage system shall be entirely under the control of said Committee and it shall have full power to make all necessary rules and regulations not contrary to law for the transaction of its business and the control and conduct of its officers, and the construction of said work, and it shall have full power and authority to do all things necessary in levying and collecting a special levy or assessment upon the property within said District or the issuance and disposal of special improvement bonds thereof.

## X.

Said committee shall have power and authority to employ, hire and discharge all necessary agents workman and servants necessary in the construction of said work, and to do any other act necessary to such end and not contrary to law.

## XI.

The committee on Streets and alleys appointed by the Chairman of the Board of Trustees together with the Sewer committee shall make out an assessment roll according to law and the provisions of this ordinance, containing a list of all the assessable property within said Sewerage District, with the name of the owner or owners of all property assessed if known and if unknown so designate, the description of each piece or parcel of property, the amount assessed thereon as provided by law and this ordinance and when completed certify the same to the Board of Trustees of said Village, which said assessment roll shall be made and certified on or before the 1st monday in June 1910.

The Village clerk shall give notice by two successive publications in the St. Maries Gazette the official newspaper of said Village that said assessment roll is on file in his office, the date same was filed and the time at which the Board of Trustees will hear and consider objections to said assessment roll by any person aggrieved thereby which meeting shall be held by said Board on the first monday in July 1910.

The owner or owners of any property which is assessed in such assessment roll whether named therein or designated as unknown owners may within ten days from the last publication of the notice hereinbefore provided for file with the Village Clerk in writing objections to said assessment or any portion thereof which objection must be specific as to property assessed or matter complained of and state the specific ground of such objections.

## XII.

At the said meeting of the said board of trustees for the hearing of such objections said board shall hear and determine all objections which have been filed by any person interested and pass upon all such objections and shall overrule in whole or in part or revise, correct, confirm, set aside such objections or order a new assessment of said property or make and enter an order confirming and approving the assessments objected to as originally made or as corrected or revised by said board.

## XIII.

For the purpose of providing funds for the costs and expenses of such improvement there shall be issued bonds of "Local Sewerage Improvement District No. 1" and the said sewer committee of said district shall have the power and authority to issue and dispose of the special assessment sewerage bonds herein provided for by issuing the same to the contractor or contractors constructing or building said sewer system or any portion thereof or other improvements connected therewith in payment therefor or may sell and dispose of the same at not less than their par value and pay the proceeds to such contractor or contractors or any part thereof or to its other servants, agents, workmen, or employees or any other necessary expense incident to the construction of said system. Said bonds shall be issued in the name of the Village of St Maries and be known and designated as "Special Assessment Sewerage Improvement Bonds of District No. 1".

## XIV.

Said bonds shall bear the date of \[blank\] day of December A.D. 1909 and shall be payable on or before date ten years thereafter bearing interest at the rate of not to exceed 8 per cent per annum payable annually on the first day of December in each year at the office of the Village Treasurer of the Village of St. Maries, or some banking house located in the City of New York State of New York. Said bonds shall be issued in the denomination of $500.00 each and be numbered from One (1) to Fourteen (14) inclusive with interest coupons attached which said bonds shall be signed by the chairman of the board of trustees and attested by the Clerk of the Village and the coupons attached thereto shall be so signed and attested or in lieu thereof may have printed thereon the fac-simile of the signatures of such officers and each bond shall have affixed thereto the seal of said Village and each bond shall state that the principal sum named therein and interest thereon shall be payable out of the local improvement fund created for the payment of the costs and expenses of such improvement and not otherwise.

## XV.

The assessment roll herein provided for when approved shall be the assessment of the costs and expenses of the improvement herein provided for against the lots, pieces and parcels of land within said district No. 1 for which the bonds herein provided for shall be issued. The assessment so levied against each piece or parcel of land may be paid in ten equal annual installments with interest thereon at the rate of eight per cent per annum. The first installment shall be due and collectible on the 1st day of October A.D. 1910 and each annual installment thereafter shall be due on the first day of said month in each succeeding year thereafter and shall be deemed and held delinquent if not paid within twenty days from said date. The amount which shall be due and payable each year shall be one annual installment with interest thereon together with the interest on all deferred installments the whole thereof to be collected as a separate tax in the manner provided by law.

## XVI.

The Village Treasurer shall as soon as the assessment roll has been approved and confirmed and placed in his hands for collection cause to be published in the St. Maries Gazette, the official newspaper of said Village in two consecutive issues thereof that said roll is in his hands for collection and that any assessment therein may be paid within thirty days from the date of the first publication of said notice without penalty interest or costs and the owner or owners of any lot, piece or parcel of land charged with any such assessment may redeem same from all liability by paying the entire amount charged against such lot, piece or parcel of land without interest within thirty days after the first publication of such notice. And the owner or owners of any such lot, piece or parcel of land may redeem same by paying the entire assessment thereon at any time after the expiration of said thirty days after the first publication of said notice by paying all installments of said assessment remaining unpaid at the time of such payment with interest thereon at the rate of eight per cent per annum from the date on which the first installment of said assessment became due to the time of such payment. All payments of assessments hereunder shall be made to the Village Treasurer and in case same shall become delinquent shall be collected in the manner provided by law for the collection of assessments for other improvements in cases where no bonds are issued. The expense and costs of the work connected with said improvement herein provided for shall become a lien upon the lands lots and parcels of land within the said improvement district and shall take precedence over all other liens.

## XVII.

On payment of any installments the Treasurer shall issue a triplicate receipt therefor one of which shall be delivered to the owner of the property one to the Village Clerk and the other to be retained by him in his office and on payment of the entire assessment it shall be noted opposite the said tract or parcel of land upon which the assessments are paid that the lien thereon is discharged.

## XVIII.

The Village Treasurer shall cause to be published in the official newspaper for four issues thereof prior to the date on which each annual installment shall become due a notice that an installment together with interest upon the unpaid installments will be due and payable on the first day of October of said year and will be delinquent twenty days thereafter unless paid within said twenty days thereafter will be collected as is provided by law. Said Village Treasurer shall also mail a like notice to each owner of each lot, piece or parcel of land assessed provided such owner is known to him but a failure of such owner to receive such notice or the failure of said Treasurer to mail or publish the same shall not be taken or held to prevent the collection of said installment and interest as provided by law. Whenever there shall be sufficient money to the credit of said improvement district in the hands of said Treasurer sufficient to pay the interest on said bonds and to discharge the principal on one or more bonds the Treasurer shall call in and pay said bonds in their numerical order. And no bond shall draw interest after the date named in such call for the presentment for the payment thereof. Such call may be made by publication in a newspaper of said Village stating the date on which such payment will be made and that no interest will be paid on said bond or bonds after such date, or such call may be made by mailing a like notice to the owner and holder thereof if known.

## XIX.

The holder of any bond issued under the authority of this ordinance shall have no claim therefor against the Village except for the collection of the special assessment herein provided for within said local sewerage improvement district but the remedy in case of non-payment shall be confined to the enforcement of such assessment or lien on said property.

## XX.

No sewer or sewer pipes which shall be laid parallel to any water main shall be placed or constructed within six feet of such water main or pipe provided that this provision shall not apply to such place or places where the sewer pipe shall have to cross over or under any water pipe, main or conduit.

## XXI.

The sewer committee shall have full charge of the construction of said sewer system and it shall advertise for and receive bids for said construction and shall make and enter into any contract or contracts as may be necessary to the end that such system be constructed according to proper plans and specifications and shall have full power and authority to that end. It shall also cause accurate profiles, maps plans and specifications to be made of the lines of sewerage as actually built and constructed so as to show the location of the branches, man-holes, flushes, flushing tanks, depth of the sewer, and the size of the pipe used in connection therewith and any other information for the future use of the village or its officers.

## XXII.

This ordinance shall become effective after it's passage, approval and publication in one issue of the St. Maries Gazette, a weekly newspaper of general circulation published in said Village of St. Maries.

Passed first reading at an adjourned regular meeting of the Board of Trustees held on October 14th AD 1909.

Approved, October 15. A. D. 1909.

Attest: J. S. Turnbull Village Clerk. Perley Martin Chairman of Board of Trustees.
