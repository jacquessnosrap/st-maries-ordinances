AN ORDINANCE PROHIBITING CHICKEN RUNNING AT LARGE WITHIN THE CITY OF ST. MARIES, AND PRESCRIBING A PENALTY THEREFOR.

BE IT ORDAINED, by the Mayor and City Council of the City of St. Maries, Benewah County, Idaho:--

## Section 1

That no chickens, geese, ducks or poultry of any kind whatsoever shall be allowed to run at large on any of the streets, lanes or alleys of the City of St. Maries.

## Section 2

That it shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation, owning, controlling or having in his, her or its possession or control any chickens, ducks, geese or poultry of any kind and character whatsoever, to permit the same to run at large over any of the streets, lanes or alleys of the City of St. Maries, or over the property of any other person, firm or corporation.

## Section 3

Any person, firm or corporation violating the provisions of this ordinance shall, if convicted, be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and punished by a fine of not less than one and not more than fifty dollars, together with all costs of prosecution; or in lieu of the payment thereof, be imprisoned in the City Jail one day for every two dollars of such fine and costs. And the continuance or maintaining of such violation of this ordinance shall be deemed a new offense for each day on which the same is so continued or maintained.

## Section 4

This ordinance shall be in effect from and after its passage, approval and publication.

Passed by the City Council of the City of St. Maries, this 5th day of June A. D. 1916.
Approved, this 5 day of June A. D. 1916.
Dr. Harley Mayor.
ATTEST: Lais. R. Schulte City Clerk.
(Published in the St. Maries Gazette, June 9th, 1916)
