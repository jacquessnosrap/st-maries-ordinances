AN ORDINANCE VACATING A PORTION OF SPOKANE AVENUE IN THE GOVERNMENT TOWNSITE OF ST. MARIES, CITY OF ST. MARIES, IDAHO.

WHEREAS, a petition was, to-wit, on the 29th day of June, A. D. 1916, filed with the Clerk of the City of St. Maries for the vacation of those portions of Spokane Avenue in the Government Townsite of St. Maries, City of St. Maries, Idaho, hereinafter particularly described, and

WHEREAS, the mayor and City Council of said City, by resolution duly adopted, ordered that said petition should be heard before said Council at a meeting thereof, to be held in the City Hall in said City of St. Maries, Benewah County, Idaho, on the 17th day of July, A.D. 1916, beginning at the hour of eight o'clock P.M., or as soon thereafter as said matter could be heard, and

WHEREAS, it appears to the satisfaction of the Mayor and City Council of said City that more than seven days notice of the pendency of such application has been given by the written notices posted in four public places in said City, two of which notices were so posted in conspicuous places on or adjacent to the portions of said Avenue sought to be vacated, and by causing such notice to be published not less than one week prior to the time fixed for the hearing of said application, in a newspaper published in the City of St. Maries, which said notices each contained a statement that a petition had been filed to vacate the portions of said Avenue described in said petition (being the same portions of said avenue hereinafter described) and contained a statement of the time and place fixed for the hearing of said petition, and

WHEREAS, it appears that it is for the interests of the City of St. Maries, and of the residents thereof, that the prayer of said petition be granted,

NOW, THEREFOR, BE IT ORDAINED by the Mayor and City Council of the City of St. Maries:

## SECTION I.

That all of that portion of Spokane Avenue in the Government Townsite of St. Maries, City of St. Maries, Idaho, particularly described as follows, to-wit:

All that portion of Spokane Avenue, lying southerly of the northerly thirty (30) feet of said Avenue, (the entire southerly boundary line of said northerly thirty (30) feet of said Avenue being thirty (30) feet distant measured at right angles from the northerly boundary line or the northerly boundary line produced or extended of said Spokane Avenue), and extending from the westerly line of Twenty-third Street, in said Townsite, easterly to the easterly line of Coeur d'Alene Street, in said Townsite, be, and the same is hereby, VACATED.

## SECTION II.

Said vacation is made subject to and upon the performance of the following conditions, to-wit:

(a) That the Chicago, Milwaukee & St. Paul Railway Company, at its own expense, shall immediately grade, fill and gravel, in a manner set out in a certain agreement hereinafter mentioned, that portion of Tenth Street in the City of St. Maries, Idaho, and between the northerly boundary line of the right of way of the said Railway Company and the St. Joe River.

(b) That each and all of the provisions of that certain agreement, executed by said Railway Company and dated April 28th, 1917, be fully kept and performed.

(c) That the vacation herein shall become effective only from and after the acceptance of this ordinance by said Railway Company, which acceptance shall be in writing, attested with the seal of said Company and shall be filed with the Clerk of said City.

## SECTION III.

This ordinance shall be in effect from and after its passage, approval and publication in one issue of the St. Maries Gazette, a weekly newspaper published in the City of St. Maries, Idaho.

Passed its first reading at a special meeting of the City Council of the City of St. Maries, held on the 28th day of April, 1917.

Passed its second reading, upon which a yea and nay vote was taken, under a suspension of the rules, upon which a yea and nay vote was taken, at a special meeting of the City Council, held on the 28th day of April, 1917.

Passed its third and final reading, upon which a yea and nay vote was taken, under a suspension of the rules, upon which a yea and nay vote was taken, and duly enacted an ordinance of the City of St. Maries, Idaho, at a special meeting of the City Council held on the 28th day of April, 1917.

Approved this 28th day of April, 1917.

O.E. HAILEY Mayor

Attest with corporate seal:

Chas. R. Schulte City Clerk

Published in St. Maries Gazette May 4, 1917.
