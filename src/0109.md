An ordinance declaring the intention of the board of trustees of the village of St. Maries, Idaho, to improve a portion of said village by constructing therein a sewer system, defining the specific boundaries of the district in which said proposed sewer system: is to be constructed stating the general character of the same and the estimated cost of the same and providing that the cost of the same are to be assessed on all property included within such sewerage district and fixing the time in which protests against said improvements may be filed in the office of the village clerk.

## Section 1

That it is the intention of the board of trustees of the village of St. Maries, Kootenai County, Idaho, to construct a sewerage system in that portion of said village particularly described and bounded as follows, to wit: Beginning at a point at the intersection of the center line of Eighth street with the southerly boundary line of the right of way of the Chicago, Milwaukee & Puget Sound Railway, thence south along the center line of said eighth street to a point at the intersection of the same with the north boundary line produced of lot six in block Eight of West Addition to the Village of St. Maries, Idaho, according to the recorded plat thereof: thence east along said north boundary line of said lot six in block 8 to a point at the intersection of the same with the east boundary line of lots 6, 7, and 8 in the aforesaid block and addition; thence in a southerly direction along said east boundary line of said lots 6, 7 and 8 to a point at the intersection of said east boundary line produced, with the center line of College Avenue; thence east along the center line of College Avenue to a point at the intersection of the same with the center line of Sixth street: thence north along the center line of Sixth street to a point at the intersection of the same with the north boundary line produced of lots 12 and 8 in blocks 4 and 5 of the Original Townsite of St. Maries; thence in an easterly direction along said north boundary line produced of said lots in said blocks to a point at the intersection of the same produced, with the center line of Fourth street; thence north along the center line of Fourth street to a point at the intersection of the same with the southerly boundary line of the right of way of the Chicago, Milwaukee & Puget Sound Railway; thence in a northwesterly direction along the southerly boundary line of the right of way of said railway company to the place of beginning.

## Section 2 

The said district to be hereafter designated as Local Sewerage Improvement District No. 3 and to include therein all the property or properties within the boundaries stated in Section 1.

## Section 3

The general character of the proposed sewer system is as follows, to wit: A gravity system, the laterals and mains which are to be placed in the streets and alleys of said sewer system to be vitrified sewer pipe in the following places with the following sizes, to wit: Six inch pipe in Fifth street north of the north boundary lines of lots eight and nine in block four, of the Original Plat of St. Maries Townsite and south of the center line of Front Avenue; six inch pipe in Sixth street north of the north boundary line of lots eight and nine in block five of the Original Plat of St. Maries townsite and south of the center line of Front Avenue; six inch pipe in Seventh street north of the north boundary line of College Avenue and south of the center line of Front Avenue; a six inch pipe in of Eighth street and west of the center line in Front Avenue east of the center line of Seventh street; also east of the center line of Fifth street and west of the center line of Sixth street; a six inch pipe in the center line of the center line of Eighth street and west of the center line of Sixth street; a six inch pipe in Sixth street south of the center line of College Avenue and north of the center line of Front Avenue; eight inch pipe in Front Avenue east of the center line of Seventh street and west of the center line of Fifth street; a twelve inch pipe in the alley between lots nine and ten of section 23, township 46 north, range two west, B. M., north of the center line of Front Avenue and south of the southerly boundary line of the right of way of the Chicago, Milwaukee & Puget Sound Railway; a twelve inch pipe across the right of way of the Chicago, Milwaukee & Puget Sound Railway from a point at the intersection of the center line of the alley between lots nine and ten of the aforesaid lot ten and the southerly boundary line of said right of way to a point at the intersection of the center line of Fifth street with the northerly boundary line of said right of way; a twelve inch pipe in Fifth street north of the intersection of the center line of said Fifth street with the northerly boundary line of the right of way of the Chicago, Milwaukee & Puget Sound Railway south of the southerly meander line of the St. Joe River; the main outlet running into the St. Joe River to be twelve inches in diameter; the location of the pipes and the size of the pipes as herein mentioned are given for the purpose of stating the general character of the proposed sewerage system and are subject to such changes and alteration as may be necessary or proper in the proper construction and installation of said sewer system.

## Section 4

The estimated cost of said proposed sewer system is $4,200.

## Section 5

The cost of said proposed sewer system is to be assessed against all property included within said Local Sewerage Improvement District.

## Section 6

Protests against said proposed sewer system shall be filed in the office of Charles R. Schulte, village clerk of said village on or before 6 o'clock p. m. on the 25th day of October, 1911.

## Section 7

This ordinance shall become and be in full force and effect from and after its passage, approval and publication of one issue of the St. Maries Gazette, a weekly newspaper of general circulation published in St. Maries, Kootenai County, Idaho.


Approved October 2nd, A.D. 1911.
O.E. Hailey
Chairman of the Board of Trustees

Attest
Chas R. Schulte
Village Clerk
