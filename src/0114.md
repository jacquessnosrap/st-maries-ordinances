An ordinance providing for the regulation and conduct of saloons, within the Village of St. Maries, Kootenai county, Idaho;

providing the time of opening and closing the same;

the manner in which same shall be curtained and screened;

providing that no chairs, tables, billiard or pool tables shall be permitted therein and that no games of chance of any character shall be allowed or played therein;

providing for the manner in which liquors may be stored in connection with said saloon;

providing that no free lunches shall be served therein;

providing the manner in which a restaurant in connection with a saloon shall be conducted;

providing that no minors shall be allowed in in such soloon;

providing for the removal of beer kegs, barrels, and other empty packages from said place of business;

providing against the sale of intoxicating liquor to intoxicated persons and other persons prohibited from purchasing liquor by the statutes of the State of Idaho;

providing that a copy of this ordinance be kept posted in each saloon within said Village;

defining the terms saloon and proprietor within the meaning of this ordinance and providing that no music be allowed or entertainment be conducted in said saloon and providing for one all night light therein.

Be it ordained by the chairman and the board of trustees of the village of St. Maries, Kootenai County State of Idaho.

## Section 1

That it shall be unlawful for any person, persons, firm or corporation to whom a saloon license may hereafter be granted by the chairman and board of trustees of said village, to conduct a saloon therein, to open said saloon or permit same to be opened for business before the hour of 6 o'clock A. M. in the morning: nor to allow said saloon to remain open for business later than 11 o'clock P. M. in the evening, on any business day, or to permit said saloon to be open for business on any Sunday, or during the hours which it is not permitted to transact business for any purpose, unless it be for the purpose of inaking some necessary repair or sweeping, mopping or cleaning said place and that during the time that any person is in there for such purpose no liquor shall be disposed of or taken from the stock of goods therein.

## Section 6

It shall be unlawful for the proprietor of any saloon, or retail or other liquor stores, to allow beer kegs, or empty barrels, or other empty packages or boxes in which liquors have been shiped, or empty packages of any kind or nature to be placed upon the sidewalk in front of their place of business, except for sufficient length of time to permit them to be loaded upon a dray, wagon, or other vehicle for the purpose of removing same from said place of busires and that only in case it is necessary to place said empty packages on the sidewalk in order to get them to or from such place of business.

## Section 7

That the term "saloon" within the meaning of this Ordinance is defined to be any place where intoxicating liquors or wines of any kini are sold to be drunk in, on, or about the premises where sold, or in a restaurant run in, connection therewith, and the word' "Proprietor" as used in this Ordinance is defined to mean any person, persons, firm or corporation conducting such saloon as defined aforesaid.

## Section 8

Any Proprietor of a saloon in said Village, who violates any of the provisions of this Ordinance, or permits same to be violated by any agent or employee, shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be fined in the sum of One Hundred Dollars ($100.00) for the first offence, and in case said fine is not paid he shall be subject to confinment in the Village jail until such fines and costs are paid at the same rate as is provided for the payments of fines im posed for misdemeanors by the statutes of Idaho and upon being convicted a second time tor violating any of the provisjons of this Ordinance, or permitting any of the provisions of this Ordinance to be violated in his place of buisness, in addition to being subject to the fine heretobefore mentioned, his license shall be revoked, and his place of business closed, and he shall not again begranted a license to sell intoxicating liquors in said Village.
