
# Ordinance 10

An Ordinance to prevent the introduction and spread of contagious diseases defining the duties of physicians and others in relation thereto, and providing penalties for violations thereof.

Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries, Kootenai County, State of Idaho.

## Section 1
Whenever any physician shall know that any person whom he is called to visit, is infected with small pox, scarlet fever or diptheria, or other disease dangerous to public health such physician shall immediately give notice thereof to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer of this Village, and if he shall neglect or refuse to give such notice, he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction thereof before any court of competent jurisdiction shall be fined in any sum not less than ten nor more than fifty dollars.

## Section 2
Whenever any person shall know that any person within his family is taken sick with smallpox scarlet fever or deptheris or any other contagious disease dangerous to public health, he shall immediately give notice thereof to the Chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer of this Village, and if he neglect or refuse to give such notice He shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction thereof before any court of competent jurisdiction be fined in any sum not to exceed the sum of TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 3
Whenever the chairman of the Board of Trustees or the health officer shall receive notice that any person within this Village is infected with small pox, scarlet fever or diptheria, or other disease dangerous to public health, it shall be the duty of the health officer to proceed to and examine such person, and for that purpose, if there be any doubt as to the nature of the disease, employ, with assent of the Chairman of the Board of Trustees, a competent physician to assist in such examination, the expanse of which shall be audited and paid as are other charges against the Village, If it is found after such examination that such person is infected with any of the diseases above mentioned or any other disease dangerous to the public health, it shall be the duty of the health officer to post upon the house where such infected person resides or remains a notice, on which shall be printed in letters not less than two inches in length, the words, "SMALL POX" "SCARLET FEVER", or such other disease as such person may be infected, and shall also in the case of small pox place a yellow flag in some conspicuous place on said premises, and in the case of scarlet fever a scarlet or red flag, and in other cases a white flag, and the Board of Trustees shall make effectual provision in the manner which they shall judge best for the safety of the inhabitants of the Village, such notice and flag shall remain upon said premises until such person having such disease shall in the judgement of the health officer be so far recovered that no danger from infection remains, and such premises have been cleaned, purified and disinfected to the satisfaction of the said health officer, and it shall be his duty to see that they are so cleansed, purified and disinfected.

Any person removing such notice or flag placed upon such premises without the written consent of said health officer shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction shall be fined in any sum not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 4
All persons who are upon the premises where such sick and infected person remains either as occupants, residents or there for temporary purposes, shall after the posting of the notice provided for in the preceding section remain on said premises unless permission be given by the health officer to remove therefrom and any such person who shall be found upon any public street or in any place where he may come in contact or mingle with people not affected with the disease with which such sick person is infected, shall be fined in any sum not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 5
Any person who after the posting of the notice mentioned in Section 3 of this ordinance shall visit such sick person or shall go into any building where such notice is erected without first having obtained permission in writing of the health officer and then only upon conditions which he shall impose, he shall remain upon such premises until such time as permission in writing is given by such health officer to remove therefrom and he shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be fined in any sum not to exceed TWENTY FIVE DOLLARS.

## Section 6
Any person bringing into this Village any clothing, bedding of other articles infected with any disease dangerous to public health, knowing it to be so infected, shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be fined not to exceed twenty five dollars, and it shall be the duty of the Board of Trustees to cause such clothing, bedding of other articles to be detained and stored in some safe place until in the opinion of the Board it be freed from such infection, provided, that if in the opinion of the said Board the storage of such property would be dangerous to the public health, then and in such case such property should be destroyed.

Approved November 6, 1902.
