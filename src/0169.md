AN ORDINANCE ENTITLED "THE ANNUAL APPROPRIATION BILL" FOR THE FISCAL YEAR COMMENCING ON THE FIRST TUESDAY IN MAY, 1915, APPROPRIATING THE TOTAL SUM OF $23,900 TO DEFRAY THE NECESSARY EXPENSE AND LIABILITIES OF THE CITY OF ST. MARIES, IDAHO, FOR SAID FISCAL YEAR, SPECIFYING THE OBJECT AND PURPOSES FOR WHICH SUCH APPROPRIATIONS ARE MADE, AND THE AMOUNT APPROPRIATED FOR EACH OBJECT OR PURPOSE; AND LEVYING A TAX OF TEN MILLS ON EACH DOLLAR OF VALUATION ON ALL TAXABLE PROPERTY REAL AND PERSONAL, WITHIN THE CORPORATE LIMITS OF THE CITY OF ST. MARIES.

BE IT ORDAINED by the mayor and city council of the City of St. Maries, Benewah County, Idaho:-

## Section 1

That the sum of $23,900 be and the same hereby is appropriated out of all moneys received from all sources and from the tax levy hereinafter provided for, to defray the necessary expenses and liabilities of the City of St. Maries for the fiscal year commencing on the first Tuesday in May, 1915, and that said sum be appropriated for the previous purposes and objects in the amounts hereinafter provided.

## Section 2

That there be and there is hereby appropriated out of all moneys received from all sources during said fiscal year, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $6,000 to be known as the Salary Fund, to be used for the purpose of paying the salaries of the city officers and employes.

## Section 3

That there be and there hereby is appropriated out of all moneys received from the ten mill tax levy hereby provided and not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $850 to be known and designated as the Sinking Fund, to be used for the purpose of redeeming all outstanding bonded indebtedness of said city upon its water works system as provided by Ordinance No. 70 of said city.

## Section 4

That there be and there is hereby appropriated out of all moneys received from said mill tax levied herein, and not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $850 to be known as the Water Works Interest Fund for the purpose of paying the interest upon the outstanding bonded indebtedness of said city upon its water works system as provided in Ordinance No. 70 of said city.

## Section 5

That there be and there is hereby appropriated out of all moneys received from all sources of said city during said fiscal year, not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $1500 to be known and designated as the Water Works Fund, to be used for the purpose of extending and maintaining the water works system of said city.

## Section 7

That there be and there hereby is appropriated out of all moneys received from all sources by said city during said fiscal year, and not otherwise appropriated; the sum of $1500 to be known as the Fire Fund, to be used for fire protection purposes in said city.

## Section 8

That there be and there is hereby appropriated out of the ten mill annual tax levy herein provided and not otherwise appropriated the sum of $300 to be known as the Library Fund, to be used for the purpose of maintaining a public library in said city and paying the expenses thereof.

## Section 9

That there be and there is hereby appropriated out of all moneys received from all sources by said city during said fiscal year and not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $6,000 to be known as the Current Expense Fund to be used for the purpose of defraying the current expense of said city during said year.

## Section 10

That there be and there is hereby appropriated out of all moneys received from the annual tax levy herein provided, and not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $1100.00, to be known as the Warrant Redemption Fund, to be used for the purpose of paying the outstanding warrant indebtedness of said city on account of warrants issued by said city and now outstanding.

## Section 11

That there be and there is hereby appropriated out of all moneys received from all sources by said city during said fiscal year and not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $2000 to be known as the Public Improvement Fund to be used for the purpose of paying for public improvements in said city during said year.

## Section 12

That there be and there is hereby appropriated out of all moneys received from all sources by said city during said fiscal year and not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $300 to be known as the Cemetery Fund, to be used for the purpose of maintaining and improving Woodlawn Cemetery now owned by said city.

## Section 13

That there be and there hereby is appropriated out of all moneys received from said mill tax levied herein, and not otherwise appropriated, the sum of $1300 to be known as the Improvement District No. 2 Street Intersection Bond Interest Fund, for the purpose of paying the interest upon the outstanding bonded indebtedness of said city as provided in Ordinance No. 156 of said city.

## Section 14

That there be and there hereby is levied upon all taxable property, both real and personal, within the corporate limits of the City of St. Maries, Benewah County, Idaho, subject to taxation for the fiscal year commencing on the first Tuesday in May, 1915, the sum of ten mills on each one dollar of valuation.

## Section 15

That it shall be the duty of said treasurer to distribute the moneys received from said city during said year from said annual tax levy and from all other sources and appropriate the same to the various funds as herein provided for and to keep a separate account of the receipts credited to each fund as expended therefrom.

## Section 16

That all ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict with this ordinance are hereby repealed.

## Section 17

That this ordinance shall take effect and be in force upon its passage, approval and publication.

Passed its first reading at a regular meeting of the City Council of the City of St. Maries, held on the 6th day of July, A. D., 1915, upon which a yea and nay vote was taken.

Passed its third and final reading, upon which a yea and nay vote was taken, under a suspension of the rules, upon which a yea and nay vote was taken, and duly enacted an ordinance of the City of St. Maries, at a regular meeting of the City Council held on the 6th day of July, A. D., 1915. 

Approved this 6th day of July, A. D., 1915.

O E Hailey Mayor. Attest with the corporate seal.
