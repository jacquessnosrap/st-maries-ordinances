AN ORDINANCE TO PROHIBIT THE THROWING OR DUMPING RUBBISH, DEBRIS, REFUSE. ETC. IN OR UPON THE PUBLIC STREETS, AND OTHER PUBLIC PROPERTY, AND TO REQUIRE PERSONS TO HAVE RECEPTACLES FOR THE RECEPTION OF RUBBISH, DEBRIS. REFUSE, ETC., AND TO DUMP THE SAME AT THE PUBLIC DUMPING GROUND, AND PROVIDING A PENALTY FOR THE VIOLATION THEREOF.

Be it ordained by the Chairman and Board of Trustees of the Village of St. Maries, Kootenai County, State of Idaho:

## Section 1 
That it shall be unlawful for any person or persons to throw or dump, or cause to be thrown or dumped, in any appreciable quantity, any rubbish, debris, refuse, slop, manure, trash, truck, garbage, or other waste matter, in or upon any of the public streets, sidewalks, squares, alleys, or other property of the Village of St. Maries except such place or property provided by said Village for the throwing, unloading and dumping of any and all such waste matter.

## Section 2
That it shall be the duty of every tenement house keeper, every lodging house keeper, every hotel or restaurant keeper, every occupant of a dwelling house, tent or other place of abode, every store keeper, shop keeper or proprietor of any business or place of business, and wery proprietor of a livery or feed stable to have a proper and suitable receptacle, or receptacles, at all times for receiving rubbish, debris, refuse, slop, manure, trash, truck, garbage, or other waste matter.

## Section 3
Every person mentioned and referred to in Section 2 of this ordinance shall empty and dump, or cause to be emptied and dumped, at the public dumping ground provided by said Village, or elsewhere so long as it is not within one mile of the Village limits, such receptacles as are mentioned and required in said Section 2, before the accumulation of rubbish, debris, refuse, slop, manure, trash, truck, garbage, or other waste matter therein contained shall become nauseous, offensive or injurious to the public health, or when directed so to do by the city health officer or by his authority.

## Section 4
The words person or persons, keeper or keepers, propr stor or proprietors, occupant or occupants, or other equivalent exp. d in this ordinance shall be construed to denote both singular and ral, and to include individuals, partnerships, associations and cor- ations.

## Section 5
Any person violating any of the provisions of this ordinance shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction ereof, shall be punished by a fine of not less than five nor more than enty-five dollars, together with costs, or, in default of payment of th fine and costs, imprisoned in the city jail not less than one day more than ten days.

## Section 6
This ordinance shall take effect five days after its ssage, approval and publication.

Passed by the Board of Trustees this 12 day of April A.D. 1910.

Approved by the Chairman of the Board of Trustees this 12 da April A.D. 1910.

Perley Martin Chairman.
